The Gold-to-Silver Ratio Just Narrowed to 62 — Here's Why

The ratio's slide signals silver outperformance. We unpack what the gold-silver ratio is telling investors now.

By GoldPriceTools Editorial Team Published 21 June 2026 1 min read

The gold-to-silver ratio — the number of silver ounces it takes to buy one ounce of gold — has narrowed to around 62, down from the mid-80s a year ago. For a metric that often gets overlooked, it carries a surprisingly clear message about where precious-metals sentiment is heading.

What the ratio measures

A high ratio means silver is cheap relative to gold; a low ratio means silver is catching up. Over the past century the ratio has swung between roughly 30 and 100, so today’s reading sits in the middle of its historical range.

Why it’s falling now

Silver is outperforming on industrial and solar demand while gold consolidates its gains. When silver leads, the ratio falls — and falling ratios have historically coincided with the most powerful phases of precious-metals bull markets.

How investors use it

Some investors “trade the ratio,” rotating between the two metals at historical extremes. At 62, the signal is less a screaming buy and more a confirmation that silver’s relative strength is real.
