Gold Price Per Ounce Today: Live Spot Rate & What’s Moving It

As of June 29, 2026, gold is trading at $84.20 per gram—up +2.1% on the week—pushed higher by persistent central-bank buying and a dollar that keeps losing ground. [goldpricetools.com feed] Both figures come from our live COMEX-aligned data feed and update continuously through each trading session.

The number plastered across financial sites is a spot price: what a buyer and seller agree on for immediate delivery. What you actually hand money over for is higher. Dealers layer a premium on top of spot, and the spread between the bid (what they’ll pay you) and the ask (what they’ll charge you) typically lands somewhere between $20 and $60 per troy ounce for standard bullion coins. Walk into a transaction without knowing that spread and you’re negotiating blind. Gold Ounce Calculator: Convert Any Weight to Today’s Value

Not everything you own is a one-ounce coin. Jewelry, dental scrap, and inherited bars show up in grams, pennyweights, and kilograms. How the math actually works: One troy ounce equals exactly 31.1035 grams (NIST). Take an 18-karat gold chain weighing 10 grams: 18k is 75% pure, so you’re working with 7.5 grams of actual gold. Troy Ounce vs. Ounce: Why the Difference Costs (or Saves) You Money

This is where buyers make expensive mistakes—quietly, and often repeatedly. There are two different “ounces” in common use, and they are not interchangeable.

UnitWeightUsed For
Troy ounce31.1035 g (NIST)Gold, silver, platinum—all precious metals
Avoirdupois ounce28.3495 g (NIST)Everyday goods: food, postal weight, hardware

The troy ounce is roughly 10% heavier than the ounce you use to weigh coffee or packages. Every major precious-metals market—COMEX, the London Bullion Market Association (LBMA), and the U.S. Mint—quotes prices exclusively in troy ounces. That’s not an industry quirk; it’s a regulatory standard.

Where did the name come from? The troy system traces back to the medieval trading fairs of Troyes, France, where merchants needed a standardized weight for precious metals to keep commerce moving across Europe. It was eventually codified into English law and carried over when the U.S. Mint was established.

The dollar-value error, put in concrete terms: Say gold is quoted at $84.20 per gram [goldpricetools.com feed] and you assume a “pound” of gold means 16 avoirdupois ounces—that’s 453.592 grams. A troy pound, though, is 12 troy ounces, or 373.242 grams. The gap between those two assumptions is 80 grams, which at today’s rate is roughly $6,736. On a single transaction, that’s not a rounding error. That’s a serious financial mistake.

One rule worth tattooing somewhere visible: whenever gold is quoted “per ounce,” it means per troy ounce, every time, without exception. If a dealer ever quotes you differently, ask for written clarification before any money moves.
