Weekly Wrap: Gold Eases, Silver Shines, Ratio Falls

Your 60-second read on the precious-metals moves that mattered this week and the levels to watch next.

By GoldPriceTools Editorial Team Published 25 June 2026 1 min read

A quick run-through of the week in precious metals — what moved, why, and what to keep an eye on.

The week in 60 seconds

  • Gold held above $4,500, easing slightly as real yields ticked up but finding steady support from central-bank buying.
  • Silver stole the show, jumping ~4% on industrial and solar demand.
  • The gold-to-silver ratio narrowed toward 62, a sign of broadening strength.

Levels to watch next week

Gold support sits at $4,460–$4,480 with resistance near $4,620. For silver, the key is whether it can hold its breakout. The next inflation print is the macro event most likely to set the tone.

Bottom line

A constructive week: gold consolidating, silver leading, and the ratio confirming the move. Nothing here changes the bigger picture of a resilient precious-metals bid.
