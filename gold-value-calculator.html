How to Work Out How Much Your Gold Is Worth

  1. Weigh your gold in grams. Use digital scales for accuracy — small errors in weight noticeably change the value. Weigh pieces of different karats separately. Kitchen scales are rarely precise enough.
  2. Select the karat. Check the hallmark stamp (375 = 9K, 417 = 10K, 585 = 14K, 750 = 18K, 916 = 22K, 999 = 24K). If there is no stamp, a testing kit or acid test can confirm purity.
  3. Add more pieces if needed. Use the “+ Add another item” button to total a whole collection — a drawer of rings, chains and odd earrings.
  4. Read the live value. Your gold value is shown in USD (primary) with live GBP, EUR and INR conversions at ECB reference rates, calculated from the current LBMA spot price.

How Gold Value Is Calculated

The value of any gold item comes from three numbers: the weight in grams, the gold purity as a decimal, and the live spot price per gram of pure 24K gold. USD is the global benchmark — GBP, EUR and INR figures are derived using live ECB reference rates.

Gold value (USD) = Weight (g) × Purity (decimal) × Spot price per gram of 24K gold (USD)

For example, a 10g 18K (750) chain at a live spot of $3,300 per troy ounce (about $106.10 per gram of 24K) is worth:

10 × 0.75 × $106.10 = $795.75 (roughly £628 / €730 at typical ECB rates).

This is the full market value of the pure gold content. If you intend to sell, dealers pay a percentage of it — see the scrap gold calculator for typical payout ranges.

How Much Is 1 Gram, 10 Grams or 100 Grams of Gold Worth?

The value of gold scales directly with weight, so once you know the price of one gram you can work out any amount. At 24K (pure gold), 10 grams is worth 10× the 1-gram price, 100 grams is worth 100×, and 1 kilo (1,000 g) is worth 1,000×. The live table above shows each of these amounts across the common karats.

Karat lowers the value in proportion to purity. The same weight of 18K gold is worth 75% of the 24K figure, 14K is worth 58.33%, and 9K — common in UK jewellery — is worth just 37.5%. That is why a heavy 9K chain can be worth less than a smaller 18K piece.

Gold Value by Weight Unit: Grams, Ounces & Kilos

Gold is traded by the troy ounce, but jewellery is usually weighed in grams. To convert between the units used in the table and calculator:

  • 1 troy ounce = 31.1035 grams — the standard unit for spot prices. A troy ounce is heavier than the everyday avoirdupois ounce (28.35 g).
  • 1 kilo = 1,000 grams ≈ 32.15 troy ounces — the unit for larger investment bars.
  • 1 pennyweight (dwt) = 1.5552 grams — still used by some US jewellers and dental gold buyers.
  • 1 tola = 11.6638 grams — common across South Asian gold markets.

Enter the weight in grams in the calculator above. If your scales read in ounces or pennyweight, multiply by the figure above to convert to grams first.

Find Your Karat From the Hallmark

To value your gold you need its karat, which is stamped on the piece as a hallmark — usually inside a ring band, on a clasp, or on a chain tag. The fineness numbers are:

  • 375 — 9 karat gold (37.5% pure). The most common karat for everyday UK jewellery.
  • 417 — 10 karat gold (41.7% pure). The US minimum to be sold as “gold”.
  • 585 — 14 karat gold (58.5% pure). Popular in US and European jewellery.
  • 750 — 18 karat gold (75% pure). Common in fine jewellery and Swiss watches.
  • 916 — 22 karat gold (91.7% pure). Traditional in Asian and Middle Eastern jewellery.
  • 999 — 24 karat gold (99.9% pure). Investment bullion coins and bars.

No hallmark, or an unclear stamp? A jeweller can confirm purity with an acid or XRF test — see our guide to testing whether gold is real.

What Affects How Much Your Gold Is Worth

  • The live spot price. Gold trades 24 hours a day, so the value here moves minute to minute. Figures refresh every 60 seconds.
  • Weight. Value scales directly with grams — accurate scales matter.
  • Karat (purity). Higher karat means more pure gold per gram, and a higher value.
  • The exchange rate. The spot price is in USD, so if you value in GBP, EUR or INR, currency moves affect your local figure.
  • Gems and fittings. Stones, clasps and non-gold parts have no melt value — weigh the gold separately where you can.

Remember this is the market value of the metal. A finished piece may sell for more thanks to its craftsmanship, while a dealer will pay less than melt. For collectible coins, condition and rarity can add a premium over the gold content.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is 1 gram of gold worth?

1 gram of pure 24K gold is worth the live spot price per troy ounce divided by 31.1035. Lower karats are worth proportionally less — 22K is 91.67%, 18K is 75%, 14K is 58.33% and 9K is 37.5% of the 24K value. Enter 1 gram in the calculator above for the exact live figure in USD, GBP, EUR and INR.

How much is 100 grams of gold worth?

100 grams of 24K gold is worth 100 times the live gold price per gram. For other karats, multiply by the purity — for example 100g of 18K = 100 × 0.75 × the 24K per-gram price. The value-by-weight table above shows 100 grams across every common karat, updated live.

How much is 1 ounce of gold worth?

The headline gold “spot price” is the value of 1 troy ounce of pure gold, which is 31.1035 grams. Note this is heavier than the everyday (avoirdupois) ounce of 28.35 grams. Enter 31.1035 g in the calculator, or read the “1 troy ounce” row of the table above, for the live value.

How do I work out how much my gold is worth?

Multiply the weight in grams by the karat purity as a decimal, then by the live 24K gold price per gram: value = weight (g) × purity × spot price per gram. This calculator does it automatically from the live LBMA spot price and converts to GBP, EUR and INR.

Is the gold value the same as what a dealer will pay?

No. This is the full market (melt) value of the pure gold content at the live spot price. Dealers and pawnbrokers pay a percentage of it — typically 70–92% — to cover refining and margin. Use the figure here as the benchmark, and the scrap gold calculator for payout estimates.

Does my gold’s value change during the day?

Yes. Gold trades around the clock on global markets, so the value moves continuously. This page re-fetches the LBMA spot price every 60 seconds, so the calculator and value-by-weight table always reflect the current market.
