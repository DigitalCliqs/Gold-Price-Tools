What Is 10K Gold?

10K gold contains 10 parts pure gold out of 24 — a purity of 41.67%. It is the United States' minimum legal gold standard, codified by the FTC's Jewelry Guides under 16 CFR Part 23. On US and European pieces it is stamped 417; on US pieces, also 10K or 10KT. The remaining 58.33% is an alloy of copper, silver and zinc — the trade-off that makes 10K harder, more scratch-resistant, and less expensive than 14K or 18K.

10K gold is worth approximately the live price shown above per gram today. This value is calculated by multiplying the live 24K spot price by 0.4167, which represents the 41.67% gold purity of 10 karat gold. Dealers typically pay 65–90% of this melt value when buying scrap, depending on the channel.

“10K is the legal floor for gold in the United States. Anything below 41.67% pure cannot be sold as ‘gold’ under federal law.” — FTC Jewelry Guides, 16 CFR Part 23

How the 10K Price Is Calculated

10K gold has no spot market of its own — only pure (24K) gold trades on the LBMA. The per-gram value is derived from the 24K spot price using a simple formula:

Formula
10K price/g = spot $/oz ÷ 31.1035 × 0.4167

The two constants are fixed: 31.1035 g per troy ounce, 0.4167 for 41.67% purity. Only the spot price changes. We fetch it every 60 seconds from gold-api.com, then convert to GBP, EUR and INR using live FX from the Frankfurter rate API.

Example at $3,300/oz spot:

  • Pure gold per gram = $3,300 ÷ 31.1035 = $106.10/g
  • 10K per gram = $106.10 × 0.4167 = $44.21/g
  • That is the melt value. Dealers pay a percentage of it (see calculator above).

The 417 Hallmark

In US jewellery, 10K is most commonly stamped 10K or 10KT. In Europe and on import-ready pieces, the same metal is marked 417 — the millesimal fineness (417 parts pure gold per 1000). Both marks describe the same alloy; only the convention differs by market.

In the UK, where 9K (375) is the local minimum, any 10K piece sold over 1 g must still pass through an approved Assay Office — London, Birmingham, Sheffield or Edinburgh — under the Hallmarking Act 1973. When appraising estate or imported jewellery, look for the 417 stamp before relying on a value calculation.

FTC Legal Threshold — The US Floor for "Gold"

16 CFR Part 23

Under the Federal Trade Commission's Jewelry Guides, no item may be labelled, advertised or sold as “gold” in the United States unless it contains at least 10 karats (41.67%) of pure gold. Items below this threshold must be sold as “gold-plated”, “gold-filled”, or “gold-tone”.

This is a stricter floor than the United Kingdom (9K / 375) but more permissive than France and Italy (18K / 750). It explains why 10K is the dominant entry-tier gold across North American retail — Walmart, Kay, Zales, JCPenney and Macy's all stock extensive 10K ranges.

Why 10K Is the Most Durable Karat

Pure 24K gold is remarkably soft — it can be marked with a fingernail. Alloy metals dramatically increase hardness. On the Vickers (HV) scale, 10K sits at the top of the standard gold-alloy range, making it the natural choice for jewellery that takes daily abuse: class rings, men's wedding bands, working chains, and children's pieces.

10K Gold and Skin Sensitivity

Because 10K contains 58.33% alloy metals (more alloy than gold), it carries a higher risk of contact dermatitis than 14K or 18K — particularly for people with nickel sensitivity. White 10K gold historically uses nickel as a whitening agent, one of the most common jewellery allergens. If you have sensitive skin:

  • Choose yellow or rose 10K — typically uses copper and silver, which trigger fewer reactions than nickel.
  • Ask for nickel-free alloys — some US manufacturers offer palladium-whitened 10K.
  • Step up to 14K or 18K — less alloy content, gentler on the skin.
  • Clear nail polish on the inside of a ring is a short-term barrier — effective for hours, not days.

10K vs 9K — US vs UK

10K (US) and 9K (UK) both serve as entry-level gold — but they sit on opposite sides of the Atlantic legal divide. The 4.2 percentage-point gap in purity has real consequences for value, resale and reach.

Resale in the US: 10K resells freely because dealers expect it — you're paid on weight and purity regardless. In the UK, 10K is rare on the high street (9K dominates the entry tier and 14K covers the mid range), but reputable scrap buyers will still test and pay melt-percentage. In France or Italy, neither 9K nor 10K legally qualifies as “gold” — sales are restricted to 18K (750) and above.

Selling 10K Scrap

10K dominates the US scrap gold market for the same reasons it dominates the high street. But the economics differ from higher karats:

  • Lower per-gram value. A gram of 10K is worth roughly half a gram of 18K. A typical 15–25 g 10K chain has a melt value of several hundred dollars at current spot prices — use the calculator above for the exact figure.
  • Higher refining cost. Refiners must process more alloy per gram of recovered gold, so the buy-rate (% of melt) on 10K is typically 3–5 percentage points below 18K or 22K.
  • Wide channel spread. The best and worst quotes on the same lot can differ by 30%+. Always get at least three quotes — the calculator above sets your floor.
  • Bulk matters. A 50 g+ lot is far more attractive to refiners than a single 2 g ring — accumulating scrap can lift the rate 5–10%.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the 10K gold price per gram today?

The live 10K gold price per gram is shown at the top of this page — calculated by multiplying the current 24K spot price per troy ounce by 0.4167 (10K purity) and dividing by 31.1035 (grams per troy ounce). It refreshes every 60 seconds.

Is 10K gold real gold?

Yes. 10K gold is real gold — it contains 41.67% pure gold (10 parts gold out of 24), alloyed with copper, silver and sometimes zinc or nickel for strength and durability. It is the legal minimum standard for gold jewellery in the United States.

What does the 417 hallmark mean?

The 417 hallmark indicates 10K gold with 41.67% purity (417 parts pure gold per 1000). In the UK this stamp must be applied by an approved Assay Office under the Hallmarking Act 1973. In the US, the same alloy is more commonly stamped 10K or 10KT.

How do I calculate the melt value of my 10K gold?

Melt value = weight in grams × (spot price per troy oz ÷ 31.1035) × 0.4167. Or use the calculator above — enter the weight and your currency, and it returns the live melt value plus the typical dealer buy-rates as a percentage.

What percentage of melt value do dealers actually pay for 10K?

Online specialist gold buyers typically pay 80–90% of melt; high-street jewellers 65–80%; pawnbrokers 50–65%; postal/mail-in gold-buying services 35–60%. Always get three quotes — the spread can exceed 30% on the same item.

Why does the 10K gold price per gram vary between different websites?

Small differences reflect the timing of the data feed, the spot source (LBMA, COMEX or OTC), and which FX rate is applied. GoldPriceTools fetches gold from gold-api.com and currency from Frankfurter, both every 60 seconds.

What is the difference between 10K solid gold, gold-filled and gold-plated?

Solid 10K contains 41.67% pure gold throughout the item. Gold-filled bonds a 10K layer to a base-metal core — far less gold by weight. Gold-plated has only a thin electroplated gold layer (often <0.05% of total weight) and effectively no melt value. Only solid 10K carries the 417 hallmark and has meaningful scrap value.

How do I convert the 10K gold price from troy ounces to grams?

There are 31.1035 grams in one troy ounce. Divide the per-troy-oz price by 31.1035 to get per-gram pure gold, then multiply by 0.4167 for 10K. See our troy ounce converter for instant conversions.
