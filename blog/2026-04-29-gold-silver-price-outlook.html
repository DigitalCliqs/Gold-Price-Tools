Macro backdrop

What moved the market

Three forces shaped the tape this week — a steady Fed, fresh geopolitical risk, and a structural bid from central banks. Silver added a fourth: relentless industrial demand.

Fed holds rates steady

The FOMC held on 28–29 April, citing uncertainty over trade policy's impact on inflation and growth. A rate hold is neutral-to-supportive for gold — it keeps real yields, gold's primary headwind, from rising further.

Geopolitical safe-haven bid

Escalating US–Iran tensions triggered a fresh wave of safe-haven demand. J.P. Morgan ($6,300 target) and Deutsche Bank ($6,000) both cite a structural demand thesis underpinning prices through 2026.

Central-bank buying

Central banks have bought roughly 800 tonnes a year since 2022 to reduce dollar-reserve dependency. That persistent demand has put a structural floor under gold not present in prior cycles.

Silver's industrial engine

Solar panels use ~20g of silver each; the sector consumed over 200 million troy oz in 2025. With ~70% of silver mined as a by-product, supply is slow to respond — tightening the market.

Good to know

Frequently asked questions

Will gold prices keep rising in 2026?
Gold remains supported by sustained central-bank buying (~800 tonnes a year), elevated geopolitical risk and persistent inflation concerns. After gold set an all-time high above $5,500/oz in January 2026, major banks lifted their targets — J.P. Morgan to $6,300/oz and Deutsche Bank to $6,000/oz for 2026. The main downside risk is a sustained rise in real interest rates, which raises the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding gold.
What is driving silver prices in 2026?
Silver is driven by dual demand: industrial use (solar panels and EV components) and investment demand tracking gold. The solar sector alone consumed over 200 million troy ounces in 2025 — more than 15% of annual demand. With roughly 70% of silver mined as a by-product of base-metal mining, supply is slow to respond, which can tighten the market quickly. Silver also tends to outperform gold in bull markets due to its smaller market size.
Is now a good time to buy gold?
Timing is difficult — gold pays no dividend, so returns depend entirely on price appreciation. Dollar-cost averaging (buying fixed amounts on a schedule) is generally preferred over trying to time the market. If you're concerned about inflation or currency debasement, a 5–10% allocation can make sense regardless of the current price. Always work from the live USD spot price rather than a headline that may be days old.
Why does gold perform well during uncertainty?
Gold has no counterparty risk — it cannot default, be sanctioned, or be inflated away like paper currency. During geopolitical stress, financial crisis or high inflation, investors move capital into gold as a store of value, which is exactly why central banks hold gold reserves. Gold has historically spiked 2–5% on major Middle East escalation events.
