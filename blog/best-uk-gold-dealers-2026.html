Best UK Gold Dealers 2026: Royal Mint, BullionByPost, Chards, and BullionVault Compared

By GoldPriceTools Editorial Team · 2026-05-03 · Last updated: 3 May 2026

Buying gold from the wrong dealer — one with wide spreads, hidden fees, or no buyback commitment — can cost you 3–5% before you even start. The UK bullion market has a healthy ecosystem of reputable dealers, but quality varies significantly. This guide reviews the main UK gold dealers in 2026, focusing on premiums, buyback spreads, storage options, regulatory membership, and what each dealer does best.

What to Look for in a UK Gold Dealer

Before comparing specific dealers, establish your criteria:

  • BNTA or IBMA membership: The British Numismatic Trade Association (BNTA) and the International Bullion Market Association (IBMA) both enforce codes of conduct and AML compliance. Membership is a strong credibility signal.
  • Buyback commitment: Does the dealer offer to buy back what you purchased? At what spread over spot? Buyback prices vary significantly — always check before you buy.
  • Premium over spot: The percentage above the gold spot price you pay at purchase. Lower premiums on large bars; higher premiums on small coins. Compare like-for-like (e.g., 1 oz Britannia to 1 oz Britannia).
  • Storage options: Does the dealer offer allocated vault storage? Is it truly segregated (your specific coins), or pooled (a share of a pooled holding)?
  • VAT and CGT guidance: A good dealer will flag which products are CGT-exempt and VAT-free without you having to ask.

The Royal Mint

Best for: Government-backed security, CGT-exempt coins, vault storage, and investors who want a single-stop solution. Premiums: 2–4% on 1 oz Britannias, 3–5% on Sovereigns. Storage: Royal Mint Vault at 0.5% p.a. (segregated, allocated). Buyback: Competitive but check current prices. AML/KYC: Full identity verification required above thresholds. Verdict: The safest, most trusted name in UK bullion — but not always the cheapest. Best for newer investors who value brand trust over marginal premium savings.

BullionByPost

Best for: Competitive pricing on coins and bars, free insured next-day delivery, and the largest range of international coins. Premiums: Often 0.5–1% below the Royal Mint on the same coin. Storage: Vault storage available via the Merrion Vaults partnership (allocated). Buyback: Live buyback prices displayed on the website — one of the most transparent buyback spreads in the UK. Membership: BNTA member. Verdict: The most price-competitive mainstream UK dealer for retail investors. Excellent for comparison shopping.

Chards

Best for: Numismatic (collector) coins, rare Sovereigns, and investors who want specialist knowledge alongside investment bullion. Established in 1964 — one of the UK's longest-running coin dealers. Premiums: Competitive on bullion; higher premiums on proof and collector coins. Buyback: Strong buyback programme, especially for Sovereigns and Britannias. Membership: BNTA and IBMA member. Verdict: The best option if you are buying both investment bullion and collector coins under one roof.

Gold.co.uk

Best for: Easy-to-use website, competitive prices, and a loyalty scheme (The Silver Circle). Part of the Atkinsons Bullion group. Premiums: Typically similar to or slightly below BullionByPost. Strong pricing on silver coins and bars. Storage: No proprietary vault; recommends third-party options. Buyback: Competitive. Membership: BNTA member. Verdict: Good all-rounder with strong silver selection and a user-friendly platform.

BullionVault

Best for: Institutional-grade allocated storage, the narrowest trading spreads in the UK market, and investors with larger sums (£10,000+). How it works: You buy fractional ownership of LBMA Good Delivery bars stored in vaults in London, Zurich, New York, Singapore, or Toronto — your choice. You own specific bars, not a pooled holding. Premiums: 0.5% commission on trades (one of the lowest available). No dealer spread on small denominations. Storage: 0.12% p.a. (minimum $4/month) — the cheapest allocated storage in the UK. Buyback: Sell instantly at the live market price, 24/7, on BullionVault's trading platform. Key caveat: You hold bars (not coins), so no CGT exemption. Verdict: The best cost structure for larger investors who are comfortable with CGT (or holding inside a SIPP).

Sharps Pixley

Best for: High-value buyers (£50,000+), bespoke service, and clients who want a London Bullion Market Association-connected dealer with private banking-style service. Part of the Degussa group. Premiums: Competitive on large bars. Storage: Vault storage at the London Silver Vaults and specialist facilities. Membership: LBMA associate member, BNTA member. Verdict: Premium service for premium investors. Less suitable for small or first purchases.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I look for in a UK gold dealer?

Look for: FCA authorisation or BNTA/IBJA membership, transparent pricing with clear buy/sell spreads, a physical UK address, positive independent reviews (Trustpilot), and buyback programmes. Avoid dealers that don't display spreads upfront or pressure you into quick decisions.

Are UK gold dealers regulated?

Gold dealers are not required to be FCA-regulated to sell gold — gold is not a regulated investment. However, reputable dealers voluntarily join industry bodies like the British Numismatic Trade Association (BNTA) or hold anti-money-laundering (AML) registrations with HMRC. Always verify HMRC AML registration at minimum.

Is it safe to buy gold online in the UK?

Yes, provided you use an established dealer with verifiable UK premises, secure checkout (HTTPS), and a clear returns policy. Insured delivery is standard practice for reputable dealers. Avoid deals on marketplace sites like eBay for investment-grade gold — use dedicated bullion dealers.

What is a fair spread for gold coins in the UK?

A fair dealer premium over spot for popular coins (e.g. Britannia, Sovereign) is typically 3–6% over spot for purchases under £5,000. Larger orders attract lower premiums. Spreads above 10% are excessive for standard bullion coins. CGT-exempt UK coins (Britannias, Sovereigns) may carry slightly higher premiums due to the tax benefit.
