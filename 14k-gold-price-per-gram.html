What Is 14K Gold?

14 karat gold (also written 14kt or 585 gold) is an alloy that contains 58.33% pure gold — specifically, 14 parts gold out of 24 total parts. The remaining 41.67% consists of other metals such as silver, copper, zinc, nickel, or palladium, depending on the alloy composition and intended use.

The "585" hallmark commonly stamped on 14K jewellery refers to its gold content: 585 parts per thousand (58.5%) are pure gold. This is the most widely sold gold purity in the United States and is also extremely common across Europe, Latin America, and the Middle East.

Why Is 14K Gold So Popular?

14K gold strikes an ideal balance between purity, durability, and affordability that makes it the dominant choice for fine jewellery:

  • Durability: The higher alloy content (41.67%) makes 14K harder and more scratch-resistant than 18K or 24K gold — ideal for rings, bracelets, and items worn daily.
  • Affordability: At 58.33% purity, 14K costs significantly less per gram than 18K (75%) or 22K (91.67%), making it accessible for a wider range of buyers.
  • Hypoallergenic options: 14K white gold with palladium alloy is widely used in engagement rings and is suitable for most people with metal sensitivities.
  • Appearance: 14K yellow gold retains a rich, warm colour that most buyers associate with traditional fine jewellery.

How to Calculate 14K Gold Value Per Gram

The formula to calculate the value of 14K gold per gram is straightforward:

14K gold value per gram = (Gold spot price per troy oz ÷ 31.1035) × 0.5833

For example, if the live gold spot price is $3,300 per troy ounce:

  • Gold price per gram (24K) = $3,300 ÷ 31.1035 = $106.10 per gram
  • 14K gold price per gram = $106.10 × 0.5833 = $61.89 per gram

This is the melt value — the intrinsic gold content value. Actual jewellery retail prices and scrap dealer buying prices will differ based on premiums, craftsmanship, and dealer spreads.

14K Gold Hallmarks and How to Identify Them

14K gold jewellery is stamped with one of several hallmarks depending on the country of manufacture:

  • 14K or 14kt — common in the USA and Canada
  • 585 — used in Europe (Germany, UK, Italy) and internationally
  • 583 — older Soviet/Russian standard (58.3% pure)
  • 58.5% — decimal purity marking on some certified pieces
