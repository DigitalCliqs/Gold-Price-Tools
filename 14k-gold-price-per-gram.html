What is 14K Gold?

14K gold contains 14 parts gold out of 24 — a purity of 58.33%. It is the most popular gold alloy for jewellery in the United States and is widely used for engagement rings and wedding bands. The hallmark you will see on European and UK pieces is 585. On American pieces you will see 14K or 14KT.

What is 14k gold?

14K gold is an alloy containing 14 parts pure gold out of 24, giving it a purity of 58.33%. It is the most popular gold alloy for jewellery in the United States, offering a great balance of durability, beautiful colour, and lasting value.

What does 585 mean on gold?

The 585 hallmark on gold indicates that the item is made of 14 karat gold. The number represents the gold's purity, showing it contains 585 parts pure gold per 1000, which equals 58.5% (or 58.33% standard). This is the common European hallmark for 14K.

How Is the 14K Gold Price Calculated?

The 14K gold price per gram is derived from the 24K (pure) gold spot price using the formula: 14K price/g = 24K spot price per troy oz ÷ 31.1035 × 0.5833. The spot price is set by the London Bullion Market Association (LBMA) twice daily. Our calculator fetches this price in real time and converts it to GBP using the live GBP/USD exchange rate.
