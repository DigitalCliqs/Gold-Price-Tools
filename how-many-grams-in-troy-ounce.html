Origin Story

Why is Gold Measured in Troy Ounces?

From a medieval French trade fair to the world's biggest commodity exchanges — the troy ounce survived because precision survived. Here's the abbreviated 600-year story.

Born at the Trade Fairs of Troyes, France

In the 12th century, the city of Troyes in northeastern France hosted Europe's most important medieval trade fairs. Merchants travelling from Italy, Flanders and the Hanseatic League needed a single, trusted weight standard for precious metals and gemstones. The "troy weight" system — based on a 480-grain ounce — emerged from those fairs and travelled across Europe in their saddlebags.

  • England formalised the troy system by statute in 1527 under Henry VIII
  • The US codified it in the Coinage Act of 1828
  • Adopted as the international precious-metals standard well before metrication
~1100 AD first documented use

LBMA Pricing Standard

The London Bullion Market Association sets the global benchmark gold and silver prices twice daily in USD per troy ounce — the LBMA Gold Price. Every "spot price" you see worldwide derives from this.

COMEX Futures

The NYMEX/COMEX gold futures contract (symbol GC) trades in lots of 100 troy ounces. The silver contract (SI) trades 5,000 troy oz lots. Settlement is always USD per troy oz.

ISO 31-4 Standard

The international standard defines the troy ounce as exactly 31.10347680 grams. ISO 4217 currency codes use XAU (gold) and XAG (silver) per troy ounce.

Shanghai & Dubai

The Shanghai Gold Exchange quotes the SGE Gold Benchmark in yuan per gram — but reports a troy-ounce USD equivalent. DMCC Dubai follows the same convention.

The Math

Conversion Formulas

Memorise these four and you can convert any precious-metals weight in your head.

Troy oz → grams
troy oz × 31.1035 = g
Example: 5 oz t × 31.1035 = 155.52 g
Grams → troy oz
g ÷ 31.1035 = troy oz
Example: 100 g ÷ 31.1035 = 3.2151 oz t
Kilogram → troy oz
kg × 32.1507 = troy oz
Example: 1 kg = 32.1507 oz t

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

How many grams are in a troy ounce?

There are exactly 31.1035 grams in one troy ounce. The precise ISO 31-4 value is 31.10347680 g. This is the internationally recognised unit used for pricing gold, silver, platinum and palladium on the LBMA, COMEX, Shanghai Gold Exchange and every other global commodity market.

What is the difference between a troy ounce and a regular ounce?

A troy ounce (31.1035 g) is heavier than a regular avoirdupois ounce (28.3495 g). The troy ounce is about 9.7% heavier — a difference of 2.754 g per ounce.

Gold and silver prices quoted "per ounce" always mean the troy ounce. Your kitchen scale uses the avoirdupois ounce. This is why spot gold at "$2,400 an ounce" gives 31.1035 g of gold, not 28.35 g.

How many troy ounces are in a kilogram of gold?

There are 32.1507 troy ounces in one kilogram. The math: 1,000 g ÷ 31.1035 g/troy oz = 32.1507 troy oz. A standard 1-kg gold bar — the dominant form factor in Switzerland, Singapore and the Middle East — therefore contains 32.1507 troy oz of pure gold.

Why is gold measured in troy ounces?

Gold has been measured in troy ounces since the Middle Ages, originating from the merchant fairs of Troyes in northeastern France. The troy weight system was formalised in England by Henry VIII in 1527 and has been the international standard for precious metals ever since.

The standard survived metrication because every major bullion market — LBMA, COMEX, the Shanghai Gold Exchange, DMCC Dubai — settles contracts in USD per troy ounce. Changing it would mean rewriting decades of contracts and price history.

How do I convert troy ounces to grams?

Multiply troy ounces by 31.1035 to get grams. To convert grams to troy ounces, divide by 31.1035. Example: 2 troy oz × 31.1035 = 62.207 grams. Use the Live Precision Converter above for any weight — it shows all six unit conversions plus the live USD gold and silver value instantly.

How much is one pound of gold worth?

One avoirdupois pound of gold is 453.59 grams = 14.5833 troy ounces. Multiply 14.5833 by today's gold spot price in USD per troy ounce — that's the live USD value. The "Pound of Gold" card above shows the current number automatically.

Note: bullion is never traded by the avoirdupois pound. This conversion is purely educational. There is also a "troy pound" (12 troy oz = 373.24 g), but it has been obsolete since the 19th century.

Are troy ounces used for anything other than precious metals?

Today, almost exclusively just gold, silver, platinum, palladium and gemstones. Historically the troy system was also used for apothecary measurements and gunpowder — that's why "grain" is also a unit for bullets and gunpowder loads (1 grain = 0.0648 g) — but those uses have been displaced by SI units.
