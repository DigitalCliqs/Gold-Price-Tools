Troy Ounce Explained

What Is a Troy Ounce?

The troy ounce is the standard unit of mass for weighing precious metals globally. It originates from the troy weights system used in medieval European trade, likely from the city of Troyes, France. One troy ounce equals exactly 31.1034768 grams, commonly rounded to 31.1035 g.

Troy Ounce vs Regular Ounce

UnitGramsUsed For
Troy Ounce (oz t)31.1035 gGold, silver, platinum, palladium
Avoirdupois Ounce (oz)28.3495 gEveryday goods, food, general trade
Apothecary Ounce31.1035 gHistorical pharmacy (same as troy)

A gold bar labelled "1 oz" at any bullion dealer is always a troy ounce (31.1035 g), not a regular ounce (28.3495 g). This difference of 2.75 grams matters enormously when buying or selling gold.

Troy Ounce vs Pennyweight (dwt)

US jewellers often use pennyweight (dwt). There are exactly 20 pennyweights in one troy ounce, and 1 dwt = 1.55517 grams. To compare a dealer's dwt price to a per-gram price: multiply by 1.55517. To compare to a per-oz price: multiply by 20.

How to Convert Grams to Troy Ounces

  1. Weigh your gold or silver item in grams on a precise scale.
  2. Divide the weight in grams by 31.1035 to get troy ounces.
  3. Multiply by the live spot price to get the raw melt value.
  4. For alloyed gold (14K, 10K, 18K), multiply by the purity factor (0.5833, 0.4167, 0.75).

Example: 8.5g of 14K gold. Troy oz: 8.5 ÷ 31.1035 = 0.2733 oz t. Value: 0.2733 × spot × 0.5833. Use our 14K Gold Calculator for an instant live result.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many grams are in a troy ounce?
There are exactly 31.1035 grams in one troy ounce (precise: 31.1034768 g). This is the global standard unit for all precious metals — gold, silver, platinum, and palladium.

Is a troy ounce heavier than a regular ounce?
Yes. A troy ounce (31.1035 g) is heavier than a regular avoirdupois ounce (28.3495 g) by approximately 2.75 grams, or about 9.7%. All precious metal spot prices are in troy ounces.
How many troy ounces in 1 gram of gold?

What is a pennyweight (dwt) in grams?
One pennyweight = 1.55517 grams. There are 20 pennyweights in one troy ounce. Commonly used by US jewellers for pricing gold jewellery.
How many grams in a troy pound?
A troy pound = 12 troy ounces = 373.24 grams. Unlike a regular pound (16 oz = 453.59 g), troy pounds are rarely used in modern precious metals trading.
