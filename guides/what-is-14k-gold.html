What Is 14K Gold? Purity, Price & Uses Explained

By GoldPriceTools Editorial Team · Published 24 April 2026 · Updated 25 April 2026

14K gold contains 14 parts gold out of 24 parts total — a purity of 58.33%. It is the most popular gold alloy for jewellery in the United States and is widely used for engagement rings, wedding bands, and fine jewellery. The “585” hallmark you see on European jewellery means exactly the same thing.

14K Gold Price Per Gram

The value of 14K gold is 58.33% of the 24K (pure) gold spot price per gram. Use the formula:

14K price/gram = 24K spot price per gram × 0.5833
Example at $3,300/troy oz: 24K = $106.10/g → 14K = $106.10 × 0.5833 = $61.89/g (approx £48.89/g)

Our live calculator applies this automatically for today’s spot price. This is the melt value — what a refiner pays. Retail jewellery prices are much higher due to craftsmanship, brand, and retail margins.

Identifying 14K Gold Hallmarks

In the UK and Europe, the standard hallmark for 14K gold is 585 (representing 58.5% minimum fineness). In the United States and Canada, you’ll see 14K or 14KT stamped on items. On older European pieces, you may also see 14ct or just .585. If an item has no hallmark, a jeweller can test purity using acid testing or XRF spectrometry.

Is 14K Gold Real Gold?

Yes. 14K gold is genuine gold. The remaining 41.67% consists of alloy metals (typically copper, silver, and zinc) which change the colour, hardness, and tarnish resistance. Gold-plated items have only a thin gold layer over a base metal and are not the same as 14K solid gold — their melt value is negligible.
