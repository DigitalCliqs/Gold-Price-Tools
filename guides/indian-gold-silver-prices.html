Gold and silver hold a place in Indian culture and personal finance unlike almost anywhere else on earth. Gold is gifted at weddings, stored as household insurance, and traded on commodity exchanges on the same morning. Silver fills temple offerings, lines kitchen shelves as Lakshmi coins, and increasingly finds its way into investment portfolios as industrial demand reshapes its global story. Understanding what these metals actually cost today — and why that price changes every single day — is not just useful knowledge for investors. It matters to anyone buying jewellery, taking a gold loan, planning a wedding budget, or simply wondering whether now is a good time to buy or sell.

This guide covers live Indian gold and silver prices across all karats and weight units, explains precisely how domestic prices are calculated from international rates, details the latest policy changes that have dramatically affected what Indians pay, and outlines every practical way to invest in both metals in 2026.

03 How Indian Gold & Silver Prices Are Calculated

This is one of the most important things to understand before buying gold or silver in India — the domestic price is not simply the international price converted to rupees. Several layers are added on top.[13][7]

The standard formula
Indian Rate (₹/g) = ( Global Spot ($/oz) ÷ 31.1035 × USD/INR ) × (1 + Import Duty) × (1 + GST)

Step-by-step breakdown with current figures:

  1. Global spot price: ~$4,513 per troy oz
  2. Convert to INR per gram: ($4,513 ÷ 31.1035) × ₹84 = ~₹12,192/gram (base)
  3. Add 15% import duty (from 13 May 2026): ₹12,192 × 1.15 = ~₹14,020/gram
  4. Add 3% GST: ₹14,020 × 1.03 = ~₹14,441/gram
  5. Add dealer/transport premiums (1–3%): ~₹14,585–₹14,875/gram

The final retail figure aligns with the ₹14,580–₹15,905/gram range seen in today's market.[3][7][13]

Who sets the official daily rate?

The India Bullion and Jewellers Association (IBJA) publishes the benchmark gold rate each morning and afternoon based on international prices, the prevailing USD/INR exchange rate, and applicable duties. Most jewellers, banks, and financial platforms — including the RBI's Sovereign Gold Bond pricing — reference IBJA rates.[7][14]

The Multi Commodity Exchange (MCX) in Mumbai provides continuous gold and silver futures pricing throughout the trading day. MCX gold is typically priced 10–12% above international spot because it already incorporates import duty and other premiums.[15]

05 The May 2026 Import Duty Hike

The single biggest event affecting Indian gold and silver prices in 2026 was the government's decision on 13 May 2026 to raise effective import duties from 6% to 15%.[16][17]

The practical impact

The duty hike added approximately ₹27,000 per 10 grams to gold's fully-loaded retail cost at then-prevailing prices. Within hours of the announcement, gold on the MCX surged to ₹1,63,000 per 10g and silver jumped 6% to nearly ₹2,97,000 per kg.[19][18][17]

Why the government acted: India's gold imports were running at approximately 83 tonnes per month in early 2026 — well above the 2025 monthly average of 53 tonnes — placing heavy pressure on the country's foreign exchange reserves at a time of heightened stress from the ongoing West Asia conflict and rising crude oil costs. The move mirrored a similar duty hike India made in 2022 during the Russia-Ukraine war, and effectively reversed the 2024 Budget decision that had cut gold import duty to 6%.[20][16][17]

This duty history matters to anyone tracking Indian gold and silver prices, because policy changes can move the domestic price by ₹10,000–₹27,000 per 10 grams overnight — independent of what the international gold price does.[18][17]

07 What Drives Indian Gold & Silver Prices?

Five forces determine the daily price of gold and silver in India:[23][13][7]

1
International spot price
The LBMA price sets the global benchmark in USD/troy oz. Since India imports virtually all its gold, the domestic price moves in direct response — usually the same direction, often amplified.[13]
2
USD/INR exchange rate
Gold is priced globally in dollars, so a weaker rupee makes gold pricier even if spot is flat. The INR fell 3% q/q and 6% y/y in Q1 2026, pushing domestic prices past international gains.[20]
3
Import duty & taxes
As the May 2026 hike showed, policy can instantly change the landed cost by 5–15%. India has a long history of adjusting gold duties in response to macro pressure.[16][17]
4
Demand & seasonality
Demand peaks during wedding season (Oct–Dec, Apr–May) and festivals like Dhanteras, Akshaya Tritiya and Diwali. Bar/coin investment surges in times of currency weakness.[22][24]
5
Global macro factors
US Federal Reserve rate decisions, geopolitical crises, central-bank gold buying and equity-market volatility all move the international price — and therefore India's rate.[23]

08 Silver Price Factors in India (Chandi Bhav)

Silver's price drivers in India overlap significantly with gold's, but include some important differences:

Industrial demand

Unlike gold, approximately 50–55% of global silver demand is industrial — electronics, solar panels, electric vehicles, medical devices and batteries. India's growing solar and EV industries are increasing domestic industrial silver demand, which supports prices.[25]

Gold-to-silver ratio: Traders watch how many ounces of silver it takes to buy one ounce of gold. A high ratio (gold expensive relative to silver) is often read as silver being undervalued.[11][25]

Import dependence: India produces negligible silver domestically and imports most of its requirements, making the metal similarly sensitive to import duties and the rupee-dollar rate as gold.[17][11]

Volatility

Silver is significantly more volatile than gold. While gold rose ~75% in rupee terms in 2025, silver rose ~136% in the same period — the higher volatility reflecting its smaller, thinner market.[26]

10 Ways to Invest in Gold in India (2026)

India offers a wider range of gold investment structures than most countries in the world, from centuries-old physical forms to sophisticated digital products.[29][14]

Tangible
Physical — Jewellery, Coins & Bars
Jewellery carries 8–25% making charges (not recovered on sale). Coins & small bars have lower premiums but still 15% duty + 3% GST — an instant 18%+ over melt value. Larger bars offer the lowest premiums.[11][30][31]
Govt-backed · recommended
Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGB)
RBI-issued securities denominated in grams of 999 gold. 2.5% annual interest plus full price appreciation, 8-year tenure, ₹50/g online discount — no storage risk or cost.[14][32][33]
Most liquid
Gold ETFs & Mutual Funds
ETFs trade on NSE/BSE like shares (1 unit ≈ 1g) via a demat account — no making charges, no storage. Gold mutual funds allow SIP investing without a demat account.[11][26]
From ₹10
Digital Gold
MMTC-PAMP, SafeGold, PhonePe, Google Pay and Paytm let you buy fractional vaulted gold from as little as ₹10. Not SEBI-regulated, no cap, but fully backed by physical metal.[11]
Advanced
MCX Gold Futures
Standard 1 kg contract (100g mini); silver standard 30 kg. Leveraged and higher-risk — better suited to hedging or active trading than typical retail investment.[23][11]
SGB tax change — critical from 1 April 2026

The capital-gains tax exemption on SGB redemption at maturity now applies only to investors who subscribed directly through the RBI at original issuance and held until maturity. Secondary-market purchasers now pay capital gains tax: 12.5% LTCG (held over 12 months) or slab-rate STCG (held up to 12 months).[34][32] For long-term direct subscribers, SGBs remain highly attractive: 2.5% annual interest + gold appreciation + tax-free gains, with no storage cost.[29][32]

11 Ways to Invest in Silver in India (2026)

Physical silver investment in India takes several popular forms — many of them deeply cultural.[11][30]

Cultural
Silver Coins & Lakshmi Coins
Lakshmi coins (10g/25g/50g) are gifted at Diwali and ceremonies. Plain 999 coins come in 1g–20g sizes; commemorative coins are issued by the India Government Mint and private refiners.
Bullion
Silver Bars & Silverware
Bars run 100g to 1 kg+. A 500g bar at ₹271/g (~₹1,35,500 metal) plus 3–7% dealer premium. Silverware (chandi ke bartan) resells on metal content only — making charges aren't recovered.[11]
Cleanest exposure
Silver ETFs & Mutual Funds
Launched in India in 2022 and growing fast. Track the domestic silver price on NSE/BSE via demat — no physical handling, storage insurance, or coin premiums. Silver mutual funds allow monthly SIPs without a demat account.[11][25]
Advanced
MCX Silver Futures
30 kg standard contract (5 kg mini) for leveraged exposure to silver price movements. Used primarily by traders and industrial hedgers.[11]

12 Gold Purity in India: Understanding the Karats

India uses a variety of gold purity standards, and understanding them prevents overpaying or being sold lower-quality gold at a higher price.[8][35]

KaratPurityCommon Use in India
24K (999)99.9% pure goldCoins, bars, investment-grade bullion
22K (916)91.6% goldMost South Indian and traditional jewellery
18K (750)75% goldModern/designer jewellery, diamond-set pieces
14K (585)58.3% goldBudget jewellery, some fashion pieces
Always check the BIS hallmark

The BIS Hallmark system — administered by the Bureau of Indian Standards — certifies gold jewellery purity. From 16 June 2021, BIS hallmarking with HUID (Hallmark Unique ID) became mandatory for most gold jewellery sold in India. Always look for the BIS hallmark and the purity stamp (999/916/750/585) before buying.[7][8]

13 Tola — India's Traditional Gold Weight Unit

In much of India — particularly in jewellery bazaars, wholesale markets, and traditional contexts — gold is still weighed and priced by the tola.

At ₹15,905/gram (24K, May 2026), 1 tola of 24K gold = 11.6638 × ₹15,905 ≈ ₹1,85,500.[36][1]  10 tola (~116.6 g) is a common larger unit for wholesale and investment. Jewellers sometimes quote per tola rather than per gram — always clarify the unit before agreeing a price.

14 Is Now a Good Time to Buy or Sell Gold in India?

This depends entirely on individual circumstances — why you hold gold, what you paid for it, and what you need the money for. What the data does tell us:

The case for caution on buying
  • The May 2026 duty hike added ~₹27,000 per 10g to landed costs — a much larger premium over international prices than two weeks earlier.[19][18]
  • Gold has already risen ~64% in rupee terms over the 12 months to March 2026.[20]
  • Jewellery demand fell 19.5% in Q1 2026 — retail buyers are finding current prices uncomfortable.[28]
The case for patience on selling
  • International gold has pulled back from its early-2026 record of $5,589/oz (~₹1,75,231 per 10g) but remains elevated at ~$4,513/oz.[20]
  • The WGC outlook stays constructive medium-term, with central-bank buying, US fiscal concerns and geopolitical risk supporting prices.[27]
The pragmatic approach

For most investors: don't try to time either the global gold price or Indian policy decisions. Instead, invest regularly in small amounts through gold ETFs or SGBs — capturing average prices rather than betting on a single entry point.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Indian gold and silver prices, tola rates, the import duty, and how to invest.

What is the gold price in India today?

As of 23 May 2026, 24K gold is approximately ₹1,59,050 per 10 grams (₹15,905 per gram) and 22K gold is approximately ₹1,45,800 per 10 grams (₹14,580 per gram). Prices change daily with the international gold price and USD/INR rate. Always verify with IBJA, MCX, or your jeweller for the exact current rate.

What is the silver price in India today? (Chandi ka bhav)

As of 23 May 2026, silver is approximately ₹271 per gram and ₹2,71,483 per kilogram. Silver prices are highly volatile and should be confirmed from MCX, Groww, or Moneycontrol for live rates.

Why is gold more expensive in India than the international price?

India pays a 15% effective import duty (from 13 May 2026), 3% GST, plus currency conversion and dealer margins on top of the international gold price. These add-ons mean Indian gold typically costs 18–22% more than simple currency-converted international prices.

What is the gold price in India per tola?

At current 24K prices (₹15,905/gram), 1 tola (11.6638 grams) of 24K gold is worth approximately ₹1,85,500. At 22K, 1 tola is approximately ₹1,70,000.

What is chandi ka bhav per kg?

Silver (chandi) is currently approximately ₹2,71,483 per kilogram (23 May 2026). This changes throughout the trading day and varies slightly by city.

What is the best way to invest in gold in India?

For most investors, Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGB) are optimal — they offer international gold price appreciation, 2.5% annual interest, and (for original subscribers) tax-free capital gains at maturity. Gold ETFs are better for liquidity. Physical coins and bars suit those who want tangible assets but come with higher costs. Jewellery is the least efficient format due to making charges.

What is 22K gold used for in India?

22 karat (916) gold is the dominant standard for traditional Indian jewellery — bangles, necklaces, temple jewellery, and bridal sets, particularly in South India. It contains 91.6% pure gold, making it soft enough for detailed handcrafted work while remaining more durable than 24K.

Does the gold and silver price vary by city in India?

Yes. Rates differ slightly between cities due to local octroi/transport charges, the buying premiums set by local bullion associations, and state-level variations. Chennai and Mumbai typically have slightly higher rates than Delhi for gold, while silver shows similar but smaller divergences.

What is MCX gold?

MCX (Multi Commodity Exchange) is India's primary commodity futures exchange. The MCX gold price is the futures-market price for gold in India, which incorporates import duty and is typically 10–12% above international spot. It is a key pricing reference for dealers, banks, and institutional buyers.

What is the silver price per gram in India? (Chandi price per gram)

As of 23 May 2026, silver is approximately ₹271 per gram in India. This is the pure metal value — retail prices for silver coins and silverware will be higher due to making charges and dealer premiums.
