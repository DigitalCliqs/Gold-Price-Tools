Silver Price Per Gram Explained

By GoldPriceTools Editorial Team · Published 3 May 2026 · Updated 3 May 2026

Silver is quoted in troy ounces on global commodity markets, but most buyers and sellers of small amounts of silver — jewellery, silverware, coins — think in grams. Converting the spot price to a per-gram figure is straightforward once you understand the formula, and our live calculator does it automatically.

How to Convert the Silver Spot Price to Per Gram

The silver spot price is quoted as XAG/USD — the price per troy ounce of pure silver in US dollars. One troy ounce = 31.1035 grams. To find the price per gram:

Silver price per gram (USD) = XAG/USD spot price ÷ 31.1035
Example: $33.00 ÷ 31.1035 = $1.062/g

To convert to GBP, multiply by the USD/GBP rate:

Price per gram (GBP) = USD/gram × GBP/USD rate
Example: $1.062 × 0.79 = £0.839/g

Silver Purity and Per-Gram Prices

Unlike gold where karat is the purity measure, silver purity is measured in millesimal fineness (parts per 1,000). The most common silver purity levels are:

To find the value of a 925 (sterling) silver item: multiply the 999 price/gram by 0.925. E.g. £0.839/g × 0.925 = £0.776/g for sterling silver.

What Drives the Silver Price?

  • Industrial demand (~55% of total): Solar panels (photovoltaics), electronics, EV charging infrastructure, medical devices
  • Investment demand: ETFs, physical coins/bars, futures markets
  • Gold price correlation: Silver tracks gold broadly but with higher volatility (beta ~1.5–2x gold)
  • US dollar strength: Silver, like all dollar-denominated commodities, moves inversely with DXY
  • Mining supply: ~25% of silver is mined as a primary product; 75% comes as a by-product of copper, zinc, and lead mining

UK VAT note: Physical silver purchased in the UK is subject to 20% VAT. This means the purchase price you pay is 20% above spot. When selling, dealers pay based on spot — this spread makes the buy-sell gap large for physical silver. Consider silver ETCs or ETC products to avoid the VAT issue.

Why Is Silver Quoted Per Troy Ounce but Sold Per Gram?

The global silver market uses troy ounces for professional trading on COMEX and the LBMA. This is a historical convention inherited from the medieval Troyes measurement system used at Continental European trade fairs. However, in jewellery and retail scrap markets, small quantities are weighed in grams on digital scales, so buyers and sellers work in grams.

The conversion is: 1 troy oz = 31.1035 grams. Divide the quoted spot price (e.g. $33.00/oz) by 31.1035 to get the price per gram of pure silver ($1.061/g). Our calculator above does this in real time.

Silver vs Gold: VAT Makes a Critical Difference

A key difference for UK buyers: gold investment bullion is VAT-exempt under UK law, but silver bullion is subject to 20% VAT. This means the effective cost of buying physical silver in the UK is 20% higher than the spot-derived gram price. The 20% VAT is not recoverable unless you are VAT-registered and using the silver for a business purpose.

Silver ETFs traded on the London Stock Exchange (e.g. iShares Physical Silver SSLN, WisdomTree Silver SLVR) do not attract VAT, making them a more tax-efficient route for UK investors seeking silver exposure compared to physical bars or coins.

How the Silver Spot Price Is Set

The benchmark LBMA Silver Price is published once daily at 12:00 noon London time by ICE Benchmark Administration, administered through an electronic auction process. Throughout the rest of the trading day, prices are driven by COMEX (XAG/USD) futures in New York and the Shanghai Futures Exchange (SHFE) in China.

The silver price is considerably more volatile than gold — daily moves of 2–4% are common, and intraday swings of 5%+ occur during major economic data releases. This reflects silver's dual role as both a monetary metal (sensitive to monetary policy) and an industrial commodity (sensitive to economic growth expectations).
