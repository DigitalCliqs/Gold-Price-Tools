The Core Formula: How to Calculate Silver Price Per Gram

Every silver per-gram calculation follows the same three steps. Once you know them, you never need a website to value silver again.

Get the spot price

Find the current silver spot price per troy ounce in USD (Kitco, BullionVault or APMEX all publish it live).

Divide by 31.1035

There are 31.1035 grams in one troy ounce. Dividing gives the price per gram of pure (999) silver.

Multiply by purity

Multiply by the item’s purity decimal (0.925 for sterling, 0.800 for 800, etc.) for that grade’s per-gram price.

Price per gram (999) = Spot price per troy oz ÷ 31.1035
Price per gram (alloy) = Price per gram (999) × Purity decimal

925 Silver Price Per Gram — Sterling Silver Explained

The 925 silver price per gram is the one most people actually need, because 925 sterling is the worldwide standard for jewellery, cutlery, decorative items and most silverware.

What 925 means: the hallmark “925” (or “.925”) indicates the item is 92.5% pure silver and 7.5% other metals — almost always copper. The copper hardens the alloy and makes it practical for everyday use; pure silver is too soft to hold its shape reliably.

Sterling (925) price per gram = Spot price per troy oz ÷ 31.1035 × 0.925

925 sterling calculator — how to use it

If you prefer a manual calculation to any online tool, the process for any quantity of 925 sterling is:

  1. Weigh the item accurately (digital scales, ideally to 0.01 g).
  2. Check the current silver spot price per troy ounce (live, not cached).
  3. Apply the formula: Weight (g) × (Spot ÷ 31.1035) × 0.925.

Example. A 75-gram sterling bracelet at $74.96/oz: 75 × ($74.96 ÷ 31.1035) × 0.925 = 75 × $2.229 = $167.19 melt value. Dealers buying scrap typically offer 70–90% of melt; retail jewellers may add significant fabrication and design premiums above it.

999 Fine Silver Price Per Gram

Fine silver (marked 999, .999, or “fine silver”) is the purest commercially available silver at 99.9% content. It is used for investment bullion bars, some coins such as the Canadian Silver Maple Leaf, industrial applications and high-purity silverware.

Because 999 fine silver has no diluting alloy, its per-gram price equals the spot price per troy ounce divided by 31.1035 — with no further purity adjustment. At $74.96/oz, one gram of 999 fine silver is worth exactly $2.41. Investors holding bullion bars or fine silver coins can use this single-step calculation to check their holdings’ value at any time.

Scrap Silver Price Per Gram

Scrap silver is silver sold purely for its metal content rather than any collector, antique or aesthetic value — broken jewellery, worn silverware, damaged coins, industrial offcuts. The scrap silver price per gram is not a different number from the melt value above; it is the melt value, minus a dealer margin. Most dealers offer roughly 75–90% of melt, with the exact percentage depending on four things:

  • Volume
    Larger quantities attract better rates — a kilo beats 10 grams.
  • Purity clarity
    Clearly hallmarked items (925, 999, 800) are valued more confidently than unknown grades.
  • Dealer type
    Specialist bullion dealers usually beat pawn shops and generic cash-for-gold outlets.
  • Form
    Bars and coins (no refining needed) beat complex jewellery that is costly to refine.

Silver Price Per Gram in India (INR)

India is one of the world’s largest silver-consuming nations, and the silver price in rupees per gram is among the most-searched silver queries globally. The calculation is identical: USD per gram × the current USD/INR rate. Indian pricing also reflects an import-duty regime of roughly 10–15%, so the domestic rupee price per gram typically runs somewhat higher than the pure currency-converted international price. Benchmarks such as the Multi Commodity Exchange (MCX) publish live rates incorporating these local factors.

800 Silver Price Per Gram — Continental European Silverware

The 800 silver price per gram matters most for antique European silverware: many pieces from Germany, Italy, France, the Netherlands and other continental countries are hallmarked 800 rather than 925. 800 silver is 80% pure silver and 20% other metals (typically copper), so its per-gram value is simply 80% of the fine silver price.

800 silver per gram = Spot price per troy oz ÷ 31.1035 × 0.800
At $74.96/oz: $2.410 × 0.800 = $1.93 per gram

A 400-gram antique 800-silver tea service at $74.96/oz would have a melt value of 400 × $1.93 = $772. Antique or museum-quality pieces may command a significant premium above melt for their craftsmanship; plain or damaged items are typically assessed at or below melt by dealers.

Why Is Silver Expensive Right Now? (And Why It Has Fallen From Its Peak)

Silver hit $121.62 in January 2026 and has since corrected to the $75–$78 range. Two questions follow: why did it get so expensive, and why did it drop?

Why silver surged (2025–2026)

  • Structural supply deficit. Five straight years of demand (solar, EVs, electronics) outpacing mine and recycled supply.
  • Gold’s bull market. Silver amplifies gold’s moves; as gold broke $3k, $4k and $5k, silver was pulled higher.
  • Industrial demand. Solar, EV production and AI infrastructure created record industrial silver demand in 2025.
  • Weak dollar, low real yields. A softer USD broadened global buying; low real rates cut the cost of holding silver.

Why it dropped from its peak

  • Speculative overshoot. The $121.62 peak was driven partly by momentum and short-covering beyond fundamentals.
  • Stronger dollar, fewer cuts. A firmer USD and reduced Fed rate-cut expectations created headwinds for both metals.
  • Easing geopolitics. A late-March 2026 de-escalation reduced the safe-haven premium in precious metals.
  • Profit-taking. A 37% pullback after extraordinary gains is dramatic but within normal historical bounds.

The structural case for silver — supply deficits, green-energy demand and an undervaluation versus historical gold-silver ratios — remains intact, according to most analysts. The correction looks like normalisation after a speculative overshoot rather than a change in the underlying fundamentals.

Wholesale Silver Price Per Gram

The wholesale silver price per gram is effectively the spot price itself — the price at which refiners, major dealers, miners and industrial users transact in the OTC market and on exchanges such as COMEX. Individual retail buyers cannot access true wholesale prices; the gap shows up as the “premium” dealers add over spot:

1 kg investment bars2–4% over spot
100-oz bars3–5% over spot
1-oz coins8–15% over spot
Gram bars15–30% over spot

For sellers of scrap, dealers pay below spot — typically 75–90% of melt — because they must refine the metal and earn a margin. The gap between what buyers pay (spot + premium) and what sellers receive (spot × 0.75–0.90) is the effective wholesale cost of the dealer distribution chain.

How to Calculate Silver Price Per Gram — Step-by-Step

Whether you are valuing jewellery, old silverware or a bullion purchase, follow these seven steps:

  1. Identify the purity. Look for a hallmark: 999 / fine = 99.9%; 925 / sterling = 92.5%; 900 = 90%; 800 = 80%; 500 = 50%. No hallmark? Have it tested first.
  2. Weigh it accurately. Use digital scales reading to at least 0.1 g — jewellers’ scales to 0.01 g are better.
  3. Get the live spot price. Check Kitco, BullionVault or MeltValue.com for silver in USD per troy ounce.
  4. Find the pure per-gram price. Divide the spot price by 31.1035.
  5. Adjust for purity. Multiply by the purity decimal (0.999, 0.925, 0.900, 0.800…).
  6. Multiply by weight. The result is your item’s melt value in USD.
  7. Adjust for dealer margin. If selling, multiply by 0.75–0.90 for a realistic offer estimate.
