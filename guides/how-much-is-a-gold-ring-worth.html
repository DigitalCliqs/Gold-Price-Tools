There is a gold ring sitting in a drawer somewhere that you have been meaning to deal with for years. A plain band, a broken engagement ring, earrings from a relationship that ended. In 2026, with gold near record highs, even modest pieces carry real money — but "how much is it worth?" has no single answer.

The figure depends on five things: the gold's karat purity, its weight in grams, the live spot price, who you sell to, and whether the ring has any value beyond its metal content. 02What Determines a Gold Ring's Value

Every gold ring valuation comes down to one formula: Weight × Purity × Spot price × Buyer payout rate. Each lever is variable, and each one matters.

  • Weight — measured in grams or troy ounces, this is the most powerful lever. A ring twice as heavy is worth twice as much, all else equal. Weigh it on a digital scale accurate to 0.1 g.
  • Karat purity — how much of the ring's weight is actually gold. A 9K ring is 37.5% gold; an 18K ring is 75% gold. The hallmark stamped inside the band tells you which.
  • Spot price — the live global market price for gold, quoted in USD per troy ounce. It moves by the second during trading hours; the calculator pulls it fresh every 60 seconds.
  • Buyer payout rate — the share of melt value an offer represents. Specialists pay 85–95%; pawn shops 40–60%. Choosing the right buyer can almost double your payout on the very same piece.

Purity is the lever people underestimate most, because two rings of identical weight can be worth wildly different amounts. Here is how the gold content of each karat standard stacks up — the bars show the percentage of pure gold in the metal:

03Gold Ring Value by Karat

Here is what each karat standard means and what the gold in a typical 4-gram ring is worth at a representative spot near $4,750/oz (about $152.70 per gram of pure 24K gold). Figures are illustrative — use the live calculator for the exact number now.

9K / 9ct Gold Rings 375

Nine-karat is the most common gold standard for jewellery in the UK and Australia. It is 37.5% pure gold (375 parts per thousand — hence the hallmark), harder and more scratch-resistant than higher karats, which is why it suits everyday bands. A 4 g 9K ring holds 1.5 g of pure gold — a melt value around $229 (about $57 per gram of item). A specialist buyer paying 85–95% would offer roughly $195–$218.

10K / 10ct Gold Rings 417

Ten-karat is the legal minimum for "gold" jewellery in the United States and rarely appears in the UK. At 41.7% pure gold, a 4 g 10K ring has a melt value near $255 (about $64 per gram).

14K / 14ct Gold Rings 585

Fourteen-karat is the dominant standard for gold jewellery in the United States and much of Europe. At 58.3% pure gold it balances durability and gold content, so it is common for engagement, eternity and detailed pieces. A 4 g 14K ring holds about 2.33 g of pure gold — a melt value near $356 (about $89 per gram); a specialist would offer roughly $303–$338. A heavier 5 g band lands near $445.

18K / 18ct Gold Rings 750

Eighteen-karat is the benchmark for fine jewellery and the standard in high-street and independent jewellers. At 75% pure gold it has a warmer colour and softer feel, and it is the most common karat for higher-value engagement and wedding rings. A 4 g 18K ring contains 3 g of pure gold — a melt value near $458 (about $115 per gram); a specialist would offer roughly $389–$435. A heavy 7 g 18K ring climbs to around $802.

22K / 22ct Gold Rings 916

Twenty-two-karat is 91.7% pure gold and the standard for traditional South Asian and Middle Eastern wedding jewellery — deep yellow, rich, and relatively soft for daily wear. A 4 g 22K ring holds about 3.67 g of pure gold — a melt value near $560 (about $140 per gram); a specialist would offer roughly $476–$532.

24K / 24ct Gold Rings 999

Pure gold. Twenty-four-karat jewellery is rare in Western markets — too soft for everyday rings and easily scratched — so most 24K rings are ceremonial or collector pieces. A 4 g 24K ring has a melt value near $610 (about $153 per gram).

05How to Find Your Ring's Karat Hallmark

Before you can value a ring, you need its karat. The hallmark is almost always stamped inside the band — look with a jeweller's loupe or a magnifying glass in good light.

  • 375 = 9K (UK / Australia common)
  • 417 or 10K = 10K (US common)
  • 585 or 14K = 14K (US / Europe common)
  • 750 or 18K = 18K (fine jewellery)
  • 916 or 22K = 22K (South Asian jewellery)
  • 999 or 24K = 24K pure gold

No hallmark, or a worn stamp? A jeweller can test the metal with an acid test kit or an electronic tester in minutes. Do not guess — the difference between 9K and 18K on a 5-gram ring is well over $280.

Gold-plated, gold-filled and vermeil rings have almost no scrap value. A gold-plated ring is base metal with a microns-thin gold layer — negligible gold content. Gold-filled is better (around 5% gold by weight) but still worth a fraction of solid gold. If a stamp reads GF, GP, GEP or 1/20, the ring is not solid gold and the calculator's solid-gold figures do not apply.

06Diamond Rings: When the Stone Changes Everything

If your ring has a diamond, the calculation changes — but not always in the direction you might hope.

For a scrap gold buyer, diamonds are largely irrelevant. They pay for the gold weight only. Melee diamonds (tiny accent stones under about 0.1 ct each) are effectively ignored because they cannot be recovered economically. Even a larger centre stone is often listed as "1 diamond included" with no meaningful premium.

For a jewellery reseller or auction house, diamonds can dramatically increase a ring's value — but only if the stone is high quality, properly certified and in good condition. The factors are:

  • Certificate — a GIA, AGS or IGI grading report is essential for secondary-market value. Without one, a buyer cannot verify grade and will discount heavily.
  • Size — diamonds above 0.5 ct start to carry meaningful premiums. Below 0.3 ct, resale value is negligible relative to the gold.
  • Condition — chipped or heavily scratched stones recover only a fraction of their value.
  • Cut — round brilliants have the most liquid resale market; fancy cuts (pear, marquise, heart) are harder to sell.

Practical advice: if your ring has a diamond larger than 0.5 ct with a grading certificate, get it appraised by a specialist diamond dealer before committing to a scrap sale. The gold is worth what it is worth; the diamond might be worth considerably more through the right channel — or considerably less than you paid at retail.

07Wedding Rings & Sentimental Pieces

The metal market does not pay a premium for sentiment — which can sting when you learn a ring you paid $2,000 for is worth $250 as scrap. Understanding the gap helps you decide what to do about it.

Most rings are sold at retail with significant markups: the jeweller's margin, setting labour, brand premium, and the "occasion premium" that engagement rings carry. The gold in a $2,000 engagement ring might represent only $150–$450 of the purchase price. When you sell, you are selling raw material — not the occasion it represented.

A free auction estimate costs nothing and could be the difference between $300 and $800 for the same piece.

08Where to Sell a Gold Ring — and What Each Buyer Pays

There is no single regulated price for a scrap gold offer. The same ring with a $458 melt value might draw offers from $185 to $435 depending on who you ask. Here is the typical payout ladder, as a share of melt value:

01 Specialist Online Buyer Best 85–95%

Bars show the midpoint of each range. Specialist online buyers offer insured postal packs, quote in 24–48 hours and pay by bank transfer — usually the best price for plain gold rings.

Always get at least two quotes. Request one from a specialist online buyer and one local option, then compare each as a percentage of the melt value the calculator gives you. Any offer below 80% of melt from a buyer claiming to be a specialist warrants a second opinion. At today's prices the market favours sellers — you are not under pressure to accept the first number.

09Step by Step: Get the Best Price for Your Ring

  1. 1
    Weigh it accurately

    Use a digital scale accurate to 0.1 g. Weigh the ring alone — no packaging, no other items. The gram weight is the single most important number you need.

  2. 2
    Identify the karat

    Find the hallmark inside the band: 375, 585, 750, 916, and so on. If you cannot find one, a jeweller can test it for free or a small fee. Do not assume.

  3. 3
    Calculate the melt value in USD

    Enter weight and karat in the live calculator above. That gives you the melt value — the ceiling any buyer should approach — in USD, with conversions a tap away.

  4. 4
    Check for value beyond the metal

    A maker's mark? A certified diamond over 0.5 ct? Antique hallmarks? If yes, get an auction estimate before a scrap sale. If it is a plain, modern, unmarked band, scrap is almost certainly the best route.

  5. 5
    Get at least two quotes

    Compare a specialist online buyer against one local option, both as a percentage of melt. Any quote below 80% of melt from a reputable buyer warrants a challenge or a third quote.

  6. 6
    Accept, negotiate, or walk away

    Reputable buyers hold an offer for several days and return items free if you decline. There is no pressure to sell instantly — especially at current high prices, where the market favours sellers.

10Frequently Asked Questions

How much is a 9K gold ring worth?

A 9K (375) gold ring weighing 3 grams holds about 1.13 g of pure gold — a melt value near $172 at a representative spot around $4,750/oz. Specialist buyers paying 85–95% would offer roughly $146–$163. Heavier 5–6 g rings are worth proportionally more. Use the live calculator above with your ring's actual weight for the exact USD figure right now.

How much is an 18K gold ring worth?

An 18K (750) gold ring weighing 4 grams contains 3 g of pure gold — a melt value around $458 at a representative spot near $4,750/oz. A specialist offer of 85–95% would be about $389–$435. 18K is 75% pure gold, so it is worth roughly twice as much per gram as 9K (37.5% pure).

Does a diamond make a gold ring worth more?

For a scrap gold buyer, usually not — they pay for the gold weight only and ignore small melee diamonds under about 0.1 ct. A larger, certified diamond (0.5 ct and up) can add real value, but only when sold through a jeweller, auction house or specialist diamond dealer rather than a scrap buyer. Have any substantial stone independently appraised before a scrap sale.

How do I find the karat of my gold ring?

Look for a hallmark stamped inside the band: 375 = 9K, 585 = 14K, 750 = 18K, 916 = 22K and 999 = 24K. You may also see the karat written directly as 9K, 14K, 18K, and so on. If there is no visible hallmark, a jeweller can test the metal with an acid test kit or an electronic gold tester in minutes.

Is it better to sell a gold ring to a jeweller or a gold buyer?

For plain gold rings with no special gemstones or antique value, specialist online gold buyers and bullion dealers typically pay the most — about 85–95% of melt value. Local jewellers often pay 55–75% and pawn shops 40–60%. For antique, designer or signed pieces, an auction house or specialist dealer may offer more than melt value.

How much does a gold wedding ring weigh?

A typical plain gold wedding band weighs between 3 and 6 grams. Wider bands, heavily engraved rings and men's rings tend to be heavier (5–8 g); women's rings with thinner shanks are often 2–4 g. Weight is the single biggest driver of melt value, so weigh the ring on a digital scale accurate to 0.1 g.

What is the difference between melt value and resale value?

Melt value is the pure gold content of the ring valued at the live spot price — the baseline floor. Resale value is what a buyer will actually pay, which is almost always below melt for plain rings because the buyer needs a margin. For antique, designer or certified pieces, resale value can exceed melt if there is a collector market.

How often does the gold ring value update?

The live calculator on this page refreshes every 60 seconds using the current USD gold spot price. Gold trades continuously from Sunday evening to Friday evening, and the LBMA sets official benchmark fixes at 10:30 AM and 3:00 PM London time on weekdays. Prices are less volatile at weekends when markets are closed.
