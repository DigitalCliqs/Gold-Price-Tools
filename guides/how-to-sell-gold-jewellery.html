How to Sell Gold Jewellery — Get the Best Price

By GoldPriceTools Editorial Team · Published 24 April 2026 · Updated 25 April 2026

Selling gold jewellery is straightforward once you know the melt value of your items and understand what a fair offer looks like. This guide covers the five steps to getting the best price, the different types of buyers, red flags to avoid, and how to calculate what your jewellery is worth before you accept any offer.

Before You Sell: Know Your Melt Value

The melt value is the intrinsic worth of the gold content at today’s spot price. Dealers pay a percentage of this value — typically 85–95% for reputable online buyers or 70–85% for high-street shops. Knowing your melt value before approaching any buyer gives you a firm negotiating position.

5 Steps to Sell Gold Jewellery

  1. Calculate the melt value using our calculator. Weigh each item separately (standard kitchen scales accurate to 0.1g are sufficient).
  2. Identify the karat from the hallmark (375 = 9K, 585 = 14K, 750 = 18K, 916 = 22K).
  3. Get at least 3 quotes — one from an online buyer, one from a local jeweller, one from a specialist refiner.
  4. Compare offers as a % of spot, not just as a cash amount. The best offer pays the highest percentage of the melt value.
  5. Complete the sale securely — use Royal Mail Special Delivery (insured) for postal services; get a receipt for walk-in transactions.

Jewellery vs Scrap Value

If your gold jewellery is from a designer brand (Tiffany, Cartier, Bulgari) or has significant gemstones, the piece may be worth substantially more than its melt value. In this case, selling as scrap would leave significant money on the table. Consider auction or a specialist pre-owned jewellery dealer who can assess craftsmanship value.

Red Flags to Avoid

  • Buyers who don’t tell you the karat they tested or the weight they recorded
  • Offers quoted as a fixed “per gram” rate with no mention of karat
  • Pressure to accept immediately or “the price is only good today”
  • Any buyer who asks you to send jewellery before receiving a quote
