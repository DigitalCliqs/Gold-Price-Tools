Troy Ounce vs Gram: The Complete Guide

Published 24 April 2026 · GoldPriceTools Editorial

If you’ve ever looked up a gold price, you’ve seen it quoted in troy ounces — not regular ounces, and not grams. This can be confusing at first, but there’s a reason precious metals use their own weight system that dates back centuries. This guide explains everything clearly.

What Is a Troy Ounce?

A troy ounce (symbol: oz t) is a unit of mass equal to 31.1035 grams. It is the standard unit of weight used for precious metals worldwide — gold, silver, platinum, and palladium are all quoted per troy ounce on global commodity exchanges.

Key fact: 1 troy ounce = 31.1035 grams. This is different from 1 regular (avoirdupois) ounce = 28.3495 grams. A troy ounce is about 10% heavier than a regular ounce.

Troy Ounce vs Regular Ounce

The two types of ounce are easy to confuse. Here is the key difference:

Difference+2.754 g—

This distinction matters enormously when calculating gold values. If a dealer quotes you a price “per ounce” for gold, they always mean per troy ounce.

Where Does the Troy Ounce Come From?

The troy ounce is named after Troyes, a city in the Champagne region of France, which was home to one of the most important medieval trade fairs in Europe. The troy weight system was widely adopted by English merchants in the 15th century and was formally adopted in Britain in 1527 for precious metals. It has been the global standard for precious metals ever since.

The system persisted because standardisation in global commodities trading is critical — every buyer and seller worldwide uses troy ounces, ensuring consistency across markets from the London Bullion Market to the COMEX exchange in New York.

What Is a Pennyweight?

A pennyweight (dwt) is a unit of weight equal to 1.55517 grams, or 1/20th of a troy ounce. It is commonly used by US jewellers and scrap gold dealers. If a dealer quotes you a price per pennyweight, multiply by 20 to get the equivalent troy ounce price, or divide by 0.6430 to get the per-gram price.

How to Convert Gold Spot Price From Troy Ounce to Gram

The gold spot price is always quoted per troy ounce on markets and financial sites. To get the price per gram:

Price per gram = Spot price per troy oz ÷ 31.1035

For example: If gold is $3,300/oz → $3,300 ÷ 31.1035 = $106.09 per gram.

Troy Ounces in Gold Bars and Coins

Standard gold investment products are described in troy ounces:

  • 400 troy oz — the Good Delivery bar, held by central banks (12.44 kg)
  • 100 troy oz — large commercial bar (3.11 kg)
  • 1 kilo bar — 32.1507 troy oz (popular in Asian markets)
  • 1 troy oz coin — e.g. Krugerrand, American Eagle, Britannia (31.1035 g)
  • 1/2 troy oz coin — 15.5518 g
  • 1/4 troy oz coin — 7.7759 g
