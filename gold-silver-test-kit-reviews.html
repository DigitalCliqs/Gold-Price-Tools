Four ways to test metal at home

Before picking a kit, it helps to know how each method works and where it shines. Accuracy, affordability and ease of use all pull in different directions — the right choice depends on what you’re testing and how often.

05 · The Method

How to use an acid test kit

Acid testing is straightforward, but nitric acid is corrosive — work carefully. Follow these six steps and you’ll read karat reliably.

Safety first

Always wear nitrile gloves and eye protection, work in a well-ventilated area, and keep baking soda and water nearby to neutralise spills. Never apply acid directly to your skin or jewellery — only to the scratch on the stone.

1
Gear up

Put on safety glasses and nitrile gloves, and clear a well-ventilated workspace. Nitric acid is corrosive — treat it with respect.

2
Scratch the item

Firmly rub the piece against the testing stone to leave a distinct, thick line of metal. Tip: file a tiny notch in an inconspicuous spot first — heavily plated items can fool a surface scratch.

3
Apply the acid

Start with the lowest-karat acid (e.g. 10K). Apply a single drop directly onto the scratch mark on the stone — not the jewellery.

4
Read the reaction

Watch the scratch closely for a few seconds:

Stays unchanged — the item is at least the karat of the acid used.
Fades or turns brown — the gold is likely a lower karat than the acid used.
Vanishes & bubbles green — not gold (likely a brass or copper base).
5
Step up the karat

If the mark survived the 10K acid, make a fresh scratch and try 14K, then 18K, and so on. The true karat is the one just below the acid that finally dissolves the mark.

6
Neutralise & clean

Neutralise the acid on the stone with water and baking soda, then wipe it clean with a paper towel. Store acids upright in a cool, dark place.

06 · FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Are acid test kits accurate?
Yes — acid test kits are very accurate for determining the surface karat of gold. They only read the part of the metal you scratch off, though, so to rule out thick plating over a base metal you may need to file a deeper notch before scratching the stone.
Can acid testing damage my jewellery?
The acid itself doesn’t touch the jewellery — it’s applied to the stone, not the item. The only mark is the scratch you make against the stone, which removes a microscopic amount of metal. Test in an inconspicuous spot to be safe.
How long do testing acids last?
Nitric acid solutions typically last about 6–12 months once opened. As they age they lose potency, which can cause false readings. Store them upright in a cool, dark place and replace them roughly once a year.
What’s the difference between testing gold and silver?
Gold is tested by seeing which karat acid finally dissolves the mark. Silver uses a dedicated solution (often containing potassium dichromate) that turns a specific colour on the scratch — bright red for fine silver, darker red for sterling — to indicate purity.
