How the 24K gold price is calculated

The 24K gold price per gram is derived directly from the LBMA spot price set twice daily in London. Because 24K is essentially pure gold, the purity multiplier is 0.999 — the closest of any karat to the raw spot value. USD is the primary currency; live conversions to GBP, EUR and INR use ECB reference rates.

The reserve currency of metals

What is 24K gold?

24K gold is the purest commercially available form of gold — containing 24 parts pure gold out of 24, with a fineness of 99.9%. It is the only purity used for investment-grade bullion (bars and most modern sovereign coins), and the standard for LBMA Good Delivery, central bank reserves and the electronics & medical industries. The hallmark is 999 in Europe/UK or 24K / 24KT / 9999 in North America for four-nines refined product.

What does the 999 hallmark mean?

The 999 hallmark certifies an item is 24 karat gold — 999 parts pure gold per 1,000, or 99.9% fine. In the UK this stamp must be applied by one of the four Assay Offices (London, Birmingham, Sheffield, Edinburgh) under the Hallmarking Act 1973. Premium modern bullion is often refined to 9999 (four-nines, 99.99%), the standard used by the Royal Canadian Mint Maple Leaf and US Mint Gold Buffalo.

The 999 hallmark explained

In the UK, gold items above 1g must be independently tested and stamped by one of the four UK Assay Offices under the Hallmarking Act 1973. The 999 stamp guarantees the item contains at least 99.9% pure gold. It is illegal to describe or sell an item as 24K gold in the UK if it does not carry an approved hallmark or an LBMA-accredited refiner assay certificate.

24K bullion formats: bar, coin, kilo

24K is the only purity used for serious bullion. Retail bars range from 1g wallet-size pressed bars up to LBMA Good Delivery 400 oz wholesale bricks. The visual finish indicates the manufacturing process: cast bars (kilo and larger) have a rough textured surface; minted bars (smaller retail sizes) have a brilliant polished face.

LBMA Good Delivery: the global gold standard

The London Bullion Market Association (LBMA) sets the global benchmark for gold quality and trading. When people refer to the "gold spot price," they mean the LBMA price — the price at which 24K gold trades between accredited refiners, banks, and dealers in USD per troy ounce. Here is how the system works:

  • Good Delivery bars — The standard unit of trade is the 400 troy ounce (~12.4kg) bar, refined to a minimum 995 fineness. Only bars produced by LBMA-accredited refiners are accepted. As of 2024 there are approximately 70 accredited refiners worldwide, including PAMP (Switzerland), Valcambi, Argor-Heraeus, Metalor and the Royal Mint Refinery.
  • Twice-daily auction — The LBMA Gold Price is set at 10:30 AM and 3:00 PM London time through an electronic auction run by ICE Benchmark Administration. This auction replaced the historic 1919 "London Gold Fix" telephone process in 2015.
  • Clearing — The London gold market clears approximately $30–40 billion of gold trades daily through London Precious Metals Clearing Limited (LPMCL), making it the world's deepest and most liquid gold market by a wide margin.
  • Retail flow — For individual investors, 24K gold is available in 1g, 5g, 10g, 1oz, 50g, 100g, 250g, 500g and 1kg bars. LBMA-accredited bars from PAMP, Valcambi, Argor-Heraeus and the Royal Mint carry the lowest premiums over spot — usually 2–5% for 100g+ sizes.
Pricing convention
All LBMA prices are quoted in USD per troy ounce. INR, GBP and EUR equivalents on this page are computed live from USD spot × ECB FX rate — both refreshed every 60 seconds against the same timestamp for consistency. This matches the methodology used across the rest of GoldPriceTools (calculators, karat pages, scrap tool).

How to calculate the 24K gold price per gram

The formula is the simplest of any karat — because 24K is essentially pure gold, the purity multiplier is just 0.999:

24K price per gram = (Spot price per troy oz ÷ 31.1035) × 0.999

For example, if the LBMA gold spot price is $3,300 per troy ounce:

  • Price per gram of pure gold = $3,300 ÷ 31.1035 = $106.10
  • 24K price per gram = $106.10 × 0.999 = $105.99

This is the melt value — the intrinsic gold content value in USD. Refiners and dealers typically pay 95–99% of melt value when buying 24K bullion (the highest payout of any karat — refiners prefer high-purity scrap). At retail, LBMA-accredited bars carry a 2–8% premium over spot depending on bar size and mint. Use our scrap gold calculator to estimate your live USD melt value before negotiating.

Investing in 24K gold: physical vs paper

24K gold is the only purity used for investment-grade bullion. If you are buying gold as an investment (rather than jewellery), you have several options, each with distinct trade-offs in cost, custody and tax treatment:

  • Physical bullion bars — Lowest premiums (typically 1.5–5% over spot for 100g+ bars). Must be stored securely — home safe with insurance rider, or allocated vault. LBMA-accredited bars from PAMP, Valcambi or the Royal Mint are most liquid. VAT-exempt as investment gold in the UK.
  • Sovereign bullion coins — UK Gold Britannia (1oz, 24K, 9999) is CGT-exempt as legal tender. The Royal Canadian Mint Maple Leaf (9999) and US Mint Gold Buffalo (9999) are equally liquid globally. Premiums are slightly higher than bars (4–8%) but liquidity is excellent and recognisability is unrivalled. For comparison, see our gold coins vs bars guide.
  • Gold ETFs — The easiest way to get exposure to the 24K gold price without physical storage. Popular options include iShares Physical Gold ETC (SGLN), Invesco Physical Gold (SGLD), and WisdomTree Physical Gold (PHAU). Management fees are typically 0.12–0.25% per year. ETF gains are taxable in the UK (no CGT exemption).
  • Allocated digital storage — Services like BullionVault and the Royal Mint's DigiGold let you buy 24K gold stored in LBMA-accredited vaults. You own specific bars by serial number; storage costs are ~0.12% per year. Combines the low premium of bars with the convenience of digital access.
  • Gold IRA (US investors) — 24K bullion (and 9999 sovereign coins) qualify for inclusion in a Self-Directed IRA. The IRS requires a custodian and an approved depository. See our best Gold IRA companies guide for vetted custodians.

For UK investors, the choice between physical 24K and ETFs typically comes down to tax efficiency: Britannia coins are CGT-exempt while ETF gains are taxable. For deeper analysis see our Gold ETFs vs Physical Gold guide.

Selling 24K gold: what to expect

24K bullion is the most liquid form of physical gold in the world. Because refiners can re-mint it with virtually no refining cost, payout percentages are the highest of any karat:

  • LBMA-accredited bars — Sell for 97–99.5% of live USD spot at any reputable bullion dealer. Bring the original CertiCard / assay certificate; bars without provenance may be discounted 2–5% pending re-assay.
  • Sovereign 24K coins (Britannia, Maple Leaf, Buffalo) — Sell for spot to spot + small premium at most bullion dealers. Proof and limited-edition mintages can command 30–100%+ over melt depending on year and condition.
  • 1g–10g minted bars — Sell at 90–96% of spot — small sizes have proportionally higher resale spreads because the dealer cost basis is per-bar not per-ounce.
  • Pawnshops / non-specialist buyers — Typically 80–90% of melt. Avoid for 24K bullion if you have access to a reputable LBMA-recognised dealer.

Before selling any 24K item, weigh it on a calibrated 0.01g scale, check our calculator above for the live USD melt baseline, then get at least three quotes. See our guide on where to sell gold safely for vetted dealer recommendations.

Frequently asked questions

How do I convert the 24K gold price from troy ounces to grams?
There are 31.1035 grams in one troy ounce. Divide the spot price (in any currency) by 31.1035 to get the price per gram of pure gold, then multiply by 0.999 to get the 24K price per gram. Our live calculator above does this automatically using real-time LBMA spot data, with USD as the primary currency.
What's the difference between 24K, 999, 9999 and 99999 gold?
24K is the karat term for the purest commercial gold (99.9% or higher). 999 ("three-nines") is the standard LBMA fineness — minimum 99.9% pure. 9999 ("four-nines") is 99.99% pure, used for Royal Canadian Mint Maple Leafs, US Mint Gold Buffalos, and premium PAMP/Valcambi bars. 99999 ("five-nines", 99.999%) is the ultra-premium grade made famous by the Royal Canadian Mint's special Maple Leaf editions — one of the purest commercial gold products ever struck.
Why does the 24K gold price vary between different websites?
Small differences between sites reflect the timing of their data feed, the spot price source used (LBMA, COMEX, or OTC), and whether they include FX conversion at slightly different timestamps. GoldPriceTools fetches live USD spot prices from gold-api.com (LBMA-sourced) and currency rates from the Frankfurter API (ECB reference rates), both updated every 60 seconds and applied uniformly across every karat page on the site for consistency.
Is 24K gold too soft for jewellery?
For Western markets — usually yes. Pure 24K gold has a Vickers hardness of only ~25 HV (vs ~120–150 HV for 18K), so rings and bracelets in 24K deform with daily wear. However, 24K jewellery is common across South Asia and East Asia, particularly for ceremonial pieces and investment chains where the higher melt value is prioritised over durability. For everyday Western jewellery, 18K or 14K are the practical choices.
Is 24K gold a good investment in 2026?
24K gold has historically performed strongly during periods of currency debasement, high inflation, and geopolitical stress — all factors present in the 2022–2025 cycle. Central banks bought a record ~3,160 tonnes between 2022–2024, structurally supporting the price. This is not financial advice — gold can be volatile in the short term and pays no yield. For longer-form analysis, see our Is Gold Overpriced in 2026? article and 2026 prediction guide.
Are Gold Britannia coins really tax-free in the UK?
Yes. The 1oz Gold Britannia (24K, 9999 fine, struck by the Royal Mint) is UK legal tender and is therefore exempt from Capital Gains Tax regardless of profit size — including amounts above the £3,000 annual CGT allowance that taxes ETF gains. As investment-grade gold it is also VAT-exempt under HMRC's investment gold rules. The Gold Sovereign (22K) shares the same exemption. See our UK Gold CGT guide for the full rules.
How accurate are these live prices?
Spot prices on this page come direct from the LBMA via gold-api.com and refresh every 60 seconds. Currency conversion from USD to GBP, EUR and INR uses the Frankfurter API (live ECB reference rates). These are melt values — the raw 24K gold content in USD. Dealer buy prices for 24K bullion are typically 95–99% of melt; retail bullion bars and coins add a 2–8% premium over melt depending on size and mint.
Is 24K gold real gold?
24K gold is the realest gold there is — 99.9% pure, with no significant alloying metal. It is the purity used by the LBMA, central banks, the Royal Mint Britannia, the Royal Canadian Mint Maple Leaf, the US Mint Gold Buffalo and every major bullion bar refiner. The 999 hallmark certifies this purity under the UK Hallmarking Act 1973.
