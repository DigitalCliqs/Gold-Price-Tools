What Is Scrap Gold?

Scrap gold is any gold-containing material that is sold for its metal content rather than its original intended use. This includes broken or unwanted jewellery, gold coins, gold-filled items, dental gold, and industrial gold components. When you sell scrap gold, dealers pay you based on the melt value — the intrinsic value of the pure gold content — minus their processing margin.

The melt value calculated by this tool represents the theoretical 100% value of the gold content at the current spot price. In practice, scrap gold dealers pay 70–90% of melt value to account for refining costs, assay fees, and their profit margin.

  • Step 2: Identify the karat of each item — look for a hallmark stamp (e.g. 14K, 585, 18ct, 750).

Where to Sell Scrap Gold in the UK

In the UK, scrap gold can be sold to gold dealers, pawnbrokers, jewellers, or online bullion dealers. Always get at least 2–3 quotes before selling. Look for dealers who are members of the British Jewellers' Association (BJA) or the National Pawnbrokers Association (NPA). Avoid mail-in services unless they offer a guaranteed minimum price and free return if you decline the offer.

A typical 9K gold ring weighing 3 grams would have a melt value of approximately (3 × 37.5% × current price per gram). Use the calculator above to get today's exact figure.

Frequently Asked Questions — Scrap Gold

How do I calculate the value of scrap gold?
Use the calculator above: select your gold's karat, enter the weight in grams, and the tool automatically calculates the melt value at today's live spot price. For manual calculation: Melt value = weight (g) × purity factor × gold price per gram.
What is the melt value of scrap gold?
Melt value is the theoretical value of the pure gold content in a piece, based on the live spot price. It represents the maximum intrinsic value. Dealers pay 70–90% of melt value after deducting refining costs and profit margin.
How much do scrap gold dealers pay per gram in the UK?
UK scrap gold dealers typically offer 75–90% of the calculated melt value for clean, identifiable gold jewellery. For 9K gold, this means roughly 28–33% of the 24K spot price per gram. For 18K gold, approximately 56–67% of the spot price per gram.
What hallmarks identify scrap gold purity?
Common gold hallmarks: 999 or 24K (99.9% pure), 750 or 18K (75% pure), 585 or 14K (58.5% pure), 417 or 10K (41.7% pure), 375 or 9K (37.5% pure), 333 or 8K (33.3% pure). In the UK, hallmarks are struck by one of four assay offices (London, Birmingham, Sheffield, Edinburgh).
Is gold-filled or gold-plated jewellery the same as scrap gold?
No. Gold-filled and gold-plated items contain only a thin layer of gold over a base metal. They have negligible scrap gold value compared to solid gold pieces. Most scrap dealers will not buy gold-plated items at any meaningful price. Only solid gold pieces (stamped 8K to 24K) have significant melt value.
