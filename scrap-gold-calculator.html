Scrap gold calculator formula

  1. Weigh your gold in grams
  2. Identify the karat (9K=37.5%, 14K=58.3%, 18K=75%, 22K=91.7%, 24K=99.9%)
  3. Multiply weight × purity percentage × current 24K spot price per gram
  4. Example: 10g of 18K gold = 10 × 0.75 × [spot price]

What percentage of melt value do scrap gold dealers pay?

UK scrap gold dealers typically pay between 70% and 90% of the calculated melt value. Online specialist refiners usually offer the highest percentage (85-92%), while high street jewellers and pawnbrokers often pay the least (60-75%). It is recommended to compare at least three quotes.

What is melt value?

Melt value, also known as scrap or intrinsic value, is the actual worth of the pure gold content within an item based on the current spot price. This baseline value is calculated by multiplying the item's weight in grams by its purity percentage and the live gold spot price per gram.

Understanding Melt Value vs Dealer Offer

The melt value shown is the theoretical value of the pure gold content in your items at the current spot price. This is not what a dealer will pay — dealers need a margin to cover refining costs, overheads, and profit. Specialist online refiners typically pay the highest percentage (85–92% of melt), while high street jewellers and pawnbrokers typically pay the least (50–75%).

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate scrap gold value?

Weigh your gold in grams, identify the karat from the hallmark (375=9K, 585=14K, 750=18K, 916=22K), then multiply weight × purity × spot price per gram. Our calculator does this automatically with the live LBMA spot price.

What percentage of melt value do scrap gold dealers pay?

UK scrap gold dealers typically pay 70-90% of melt value. Online specialist refiners pay the most (85-92%). High street jewellers typically pay the least (60-75%). Always get at least 3 quotes.

How do I find the karat of my gold?

Look for a hallmark stamped on the item: 375 = 9K, 585 = 14K, 750 = 18K, 916 = 22K, 999 = 24K. In the UK, look for Assay Office marks alongside the fineness number.

What is melt value?

Melt value (also called scrap value or intrinsic value) is the value of the pure gold content of an item based on the current spot price. It is calculated as: weight (grams) x purity (%) x spot price per gram.
