Gold Price Per Gram vs Per Troy Ounce: Which Should You Use?

International gold markets quote the spot price per troy ounce (31.1035 grams). But jewellers, scrap dealers, and consumers work with weights in grams — creating a persistent source of confusion. When comparing quotes from different dealers, always confirm whether the price given is per gram or per troy ounce; a careless misread can result in dramatically overpaying or underselling. To convert from troy ounce to gram: divide by 31.1035. To convert from gram to troy ounce: multiply by 31.1035. UK scrap gold dealers typically advertise their buying rates in GBP per gram — which is why knowing the live GBP gold price per gram is directly actionable for UK sellers.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is a gram of gold worth today?

A gram of pure (24K) gold is worth approximately £114.72 / $148.83 today (May 2026 estimate based on gold near $4,627/oz). For 9K gold, multiply by 0.375; for 14K by 0.5833; for 18K by 0.75. The live calculator above shows the current value for all karats.

Why does the gold price per gram change daily?

The gold price per gram fluctuates continuously because it is derived from the international gold spot price, which is set by supply and demand trading on COMEX futures and LBMA OTC markets 24 hours a day. Major drivers of daily price changes include USD/GBP exchange rate moves, Federal Reserve interest rate expectations, geopolitical events, and institutional investment flows.

What is the difference between 9K and 24K gold price per gram?

24K gold is 100% pure; 9K gold is 37.5% pure. So the 9K gold price per gram is exactly 37.5% of the 24K price per gram. At 24K = £114.72/g, 9K = £114.72 × 0.375 = £43.02/g. This difference represents the value of the non-gold alloy metals (copper, silver, etc.) that make up 62.5% of a 9K piece.
