How We Calculate Gold and Silver Prices

All precious metal prices on PureMetals are derived from the live global spot price of gold (XAU/USD) and silver (XAG/USD), sourced via GoldAPI.io — an authenticated market data provider. The spot price represents the current market price for one troy ounce of pure (24K / .999) metal, traded on international commodity exchanges.

For karat-specific gold prices, we multiply the 24K spot price per gram by the karat's purity factor:

  • 24K gold: Spot price per gram × 0.9999 (99.99% pure)
  • 18K gold: Spot price per gram × 0.75 (75% pure)
  • 14K gold: Spot price per gram × 0.5833 (58.33% pure)
  • 10K gold: Spot price per gram × 0.4167 (41.67% pure)
  • 9K gold: Spot price per gram × 0.375 (37.5% pure)

The spot price per gram is calculated by dividing the troy ounce price by 31.1035, the number of grams in one troy ounce. All values are indicative market prices — actual buy/sell prices from dealers will include a spread or commission.

Live spot prices are provided by GoldAPI.io, which sources data from global precious metals markets. Prices refresh every 60 seconds during market hours. During periods when live data is unavailable (e.g. API downtime), the site displays the most recently cached price with a clear timestamp indicating when the data was last updated.
