How We Calculate Gold and Silver Prices

All precious metal prices on GoldPriceTools are derived from the live global spot price of gold (XAU/USD) and silver (XAG/USD), sourced via gold-api.com — an authenticated market data provider. The spot price represents the current market price for one troy ounce of pure (24K / .999) metal, traded on international commodity exchanges including the LBMA (London Bullion Market Association).

For karat-specific gold prices, we multiply the 24K spot price per gram by the karat's purity factor:

  • 24K gold: Spot price per gram × 0.9999 (99.99% pure)
  • 18K gold: Spot price per gram × 0.75 (75% pure)
  • 14K gold: Spot price per gram × 0.5833 (58.33% pure)
  • 10K gold: Spot price per gram × 0.4167 (41.67% pure)
  • 9K gold: Spot price per gram × 0.375 (37.5% pure)

The spot price per gram is calculated by dividing the troy ounce price by 31.1035, the exact number of grams in one troy ounce. All values are indicative market prices — actual buy/sell prices from dealers will include a spread or commission.
