Silver Price Per Kilo Explained

The silver price per kilogram is derived from the global silver spot price (XAG/USD), which is quoted in US dollars per troy ounce. Since one kilogram equals 32.1507 troy ounces, the formula is:

Silver price per kg = Silver spot price (per troy oz) × 32.1507

For example, if the silver spot price is $33.00 per troy ounce: $33.00 × 32.1507 = $1,060.97 per kilogram.

Silver Price Per Kilo vs. Per Gram vs. Per Troy Ounce

Silver is traded globally in troy ounces, but buyers and sellers of physical silver often work in grams or kilograms. Here are the key unit relationships:

  • 1 kilogram = 1,000 grams = 32.1507 troy ounces
  • 1 troy ounce = 31.1035 grams = 0.0311 kilograms
  • 1 gram = 0.03215 troy ounces = 0.001 kilograms

The kilogram is the standard unit for industrial silver buyers, refineries, and large-volume bullion traders. Jewellers and small-scale dealers more commonly work in grams. Investors and coin collectors typically use troy ounces.

925 Sterling Silver Price Per Gram

Sterling silver (.925) is 92.5% pure silver, alloyed with 7.5% copper or other metals to improve hardness. It is the most common silver standard for jewellery and silverware worldwide. The formula for sterling silver value per gram is:

Sterling silver per gram = (Spot price ÷ 31.1035) × 0.925

Sterling silver hallmarks to look for: 925, Sterling, Ster, or a lion passant (UK hallmark). Items marked “Silver Plated” or “EPNS” are not sterling silver and have negligible melt value.

Kilo of Silver Price — What Affects It?

  • Global supply and demand: Silver has significant industrial demand (electronics, solar panels, EVs) in addition to investment demand — more so than gold.
  • USD strength: Silver is priced in USD, so a stronger dollar generally pushes the silver price lower and vice versa.
  • Gold/silver ratio: The gold-to-silver ratio (how many ounces of silver equal one ounce of gold) historically averages 60–80:1. Traders use this ratio to identify relative value between the two metals.
  • Industrial cycles: Silver demand from solar manufacturing, EV batteries, and 5G infrastructure directly affects the spot price.
  • ETF flows: Large inflows or outflows from silver ETFs (e.g. iShares Silver Trust, SLV) can move the price significantly on short timeframes.

Silver Price in India (INR)

The silver price in India is quoted in Indian Rupees (INR) per kilogram and per 10 grams. It is influenced by the MCX (Multi Commodity Exchange) rate, which is derived from the international spot price converted at the USD/INR exchange rate, plus import duties and local taxes.
