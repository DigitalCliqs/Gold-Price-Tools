About Silver Pricing

Silver is priced per troy ounce on global spot markets. One troy ounce equals 31.1035 grams, so one kilogram of silver contains 32.1507 troy ounces. The silver spot price is set continuously on exchanges including COMEX (New York) and LBMA (London).

How many troy ounces are in a kilogram of silver?

There are exactly 32.1507 troy ounces in one kilogram of silver. Because one troy ounce is equal to 31.1035 grams, you calculate this by dividing 1,000 grams by 31.1035, yielding the 32.1507 conversion factor.

What Is the Silver Price Per Kilogram?

The silver price per kilogram is calculated directly from the global silver spot price, which is quoted in US dollars per troy ounce on the COMEX futures exchange and the LBMA in London. Because there are 32.1507 troy ounces in one kilogram (not 31.1035 — that is per gram), the formula is:

Silver price per kg = Spot price (USD/troy oz) × 32.1507

For example, at a spot price of $33.00/oz, the silver price per kg = $33.00 × 32.1507 = $1,060.97 per kg. Our calculator above converts this to GBP, EUR, and INR in real time using live exchange rates.

Why Track Silver Price Per Kilo?

The per-kilogram price is most useful for:

  • Industrial buyers — manufacturers of solar panels, electronics, and medical devices purchase silver in kilograms or tonnes.
  • Bulk scrap dealers — recyclers and refineries price silver by the kilo rather than gram.
  • Large investors — 1 kg silver bars (such as those produced by the Royal Mint or PAMP Suisse) are a popular investment vehicle with lower premiums per ounce than smaller bars or coins.

Silver Spot Price Sources

The global silver benchmark is set by the LBMA Silver Price, published once daily at noon London time by ICE Benchmark Administration. Throughout the trading day, the live price is determined by COMEX (New York) futures, with significant activity also in Shanghai (SHFE). 1 kg Silver Bar Prices in the UK

A 1 kg silver bar typically carries a premium over spot of £30–£80 depending on the brand and retailer. Well-known brands available in the UK include Royal Mint, PAMP Suisse, Metalor, Umicore, and Baird & Co. Key points for UK buyers:

  • VAT: Silver bullion (bars and coins) is subject to 20% VAT in the UK, unlike gold which is VAT-exempt. Factor this into your cost basis.
  • CGT: Profits on silver bullion are subject to Capital Gains Tax. Some silver coins (legal tender) may qualify for CGT exemption — check with HMRC or a tax adviser.
  • Storage: 1 kg bars are practical to store at home or in a specialist bullion vault. Professional vault storage typically costs 0.5%–1% of asset value per year.

Silver Price History: Key Milestones

Understanding historical context helps interpret current silver prices:

  • 1980: Silver hit an all-time high of ~$50/oz driven by the Hunt Brothers' attempted cornering of the market.
  • 2011: Silver reached $49.80/oz during the commodity supercycle and QE-driven precious metals rally.
  • 2020: Pandemic-era monetary stimulus pushed silver to ~$29/oz.
  • 2021: The "Silver Squeeze" Reddit-driven event briefly spiked prices above $30/oz.
  • 2024–2026: Strong industrial demand (solar photovoltaic, EV batteries) and central bank gold buying (which supports the gold/silver ratio) have kept prices elevated.

Factors Driving the Silver Price

Silver has a dual role as both a monetary metal and an industrial commodity, making its price dynamics more complex than gold:

  • Solar panel demand — Silver paste is a critical component in photovoltaic cells. The global solar energy build-out is a structural tailwind for silver demand.
  • Electronics and EVs — Silver is used in EV battery management systems, 5G infrastructure, and semiconductor manufacturing.
  • Investment demand — Silver ETFs, coins, and bars attract investment flows during periods of monetary uncertainty.
  • Gold/silver ratio — Many investors monitor this ratio to assess relative value between the two metals.
  • Mine supply — Approximately 70% of silver is produced as a by-product of copper, lead, and zinc mining, making supply relatively inelastic.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many troy ounces are in a kilogram of silver?

There are exactly 32.1507 troy ounces in one kilogram. This is slightly different from avoirdupois ounces (35.274 oz/kg) — precious metals are always measured in troy ounces, not regular ounces.

Is silver a good investment in 2026?

Silver has structural industrial demand tailwinds from solar energy and electrification, and typically benefits from the same macroeconomic factors as gold (low real rates, dollar weakness, geopolitical risk). However, silver is significantly more volatile than gold and has higher industrial exposure, making it higher risk. Consult a qualified financial adviser before making investment decisions.

What is the difference between the spot price and what I pay for a 1 kg silver bar?

The spot price is the theoretical price for immediate delivery of unallocated silver. Physical bars carry a premium to cover refining, fabrication, dealer margin, and VAT (in the UK). A 1 kg bar typically trades at spot plus £40–£80, plus 20% VAT, so the all-in cost is substantially above the spot price shown on our calculator.
