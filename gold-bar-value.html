What Is the LBMA Good Delivery Standard?

The London Bullion Market Association (LBMA) sets the global standard for wholesale gold bars. A Good Delivery bar weighs between 350 and 430 troy ounces (roughly 10.9–13.4 kg) and must be at least 99.5% pure gold. These are the bars traded between central banks, LBMA-member banks and large institutional investors, and they are the source of the live USD spot price this calculator uses.

For retail investors, the 1 troy ounce and the 100 g bars are the most popular — they offer a balanced trade-off between premium-over-spot and ease of resale. Larger bars carry lower premiums per gram but are less liquid for individual sellers.

Spot Price vs What You Actually Pay

This calculator shows the melt value — the theoretical USD value of the pure gold content at the current LBMA spot price. In practice, the price you pay for a physical gold bar includes a premium over spot that covers refining, fabrication, dealer margin and distribution costs. The premium varies sharply by size:

5–15%
Small bars (1g – 10g) High fabrication cost relative to gold content. Best as gifts, not investment.
2–5%
1 troy oz bars The most liquid retail size globally. Easy to sell anywhere.
1–3%
100 g – 1 kg bars Better value per gram for larger holdings. Widely accepted by dealers and banks.
< 0.5%
Good Delivery (400 oz) Institutional only — LBMA-member banks, central banks and refiners.

When selling gold bars, most dealers will offer 97–99% of spot for 1oz+ bars in good condition with an assay card. Smaller bars typically fetch 95–97% of spot due to higher verification overhead. Use the calculator’s premium gauge above to estimate both your retail buy price and your likely sell-back value.

Gold Bar Tax Treatment — US, UK & EU

United States: Investment-grade gold bars (.995+ purity) are treated as collectibles by the IRS, taxed at the long-term collectibles capital gains rate (currently capped at 28%) when held longer than one year. No federal sales tax on physical bullion, though state sales tax rules vary.

United Kingdom: Investment gold bars meeting the 99.5% purity threshold are exempt from VAT under HMRC’s investment gold rules. Profits are subject to Capital Gains Tax (CGT). UK investors should note that gold sovereign and Britannia coins are CGT-exempt as legal tender — a tax-efficient alternative for smaller holdings.

European Union: The EU Investment Gold Directive (98/80/EC) exempts investment-grade gold bars (995+ fineness) from VAT across all member states. Capital gains treatment varies by country.

Frequently Asked Questions

How is a gold bar valued?

A gold bar’s melt value is its weight in troy ounces multiplied by the live USD spot price per troy ounce. A 100 g bar contains 3.215 troy ounces. At a $3,200/oz spot price, that’s a melt value of $10,288 USD. The retail price you actually pay includes a small premium above the melt value.

What is the most popular gold bar size for investors?

The 1 troy ounce (31.1 g) and the 100 g bar are the most popular investment sizes worldwide. The 1 oz bar is the global retail benchmark — easy to buy, sell and store. The 100 g bar offers a slightly lower premium per gram and is widely accepted by dealers, banks and refineries.

What is a Good Delivery bar?

A Good Delivery bar is the LBMA’s standard wholesale gold bar, weighing 350–430 troy ounces (about 11–13 kg) at minimum 99.5% fineness. These bars trade between LBMA-member banks, central banks and refiners and are not generally sold to retail investors. The 400 oz size in this calculator represents the upper Good Delivery weight.

Are gold bars tax-free?

Investment-grade gold bars at 99.5%+ purity are VAT-exempt across the UK and EU under the Investment Gold Directive. In the US there is no federal sales tax on bullion but state sales tax rules vary. Capital gains tax always applies on profits when sold — collectibles rate up to 28% in the US, standard CGT in the UK and EU.

Where can I buy gold bars?

Buy from LBMA-accredited or industry-association-member dealers. Reputable global names include JM Bullion, APMEX, Kitco, Money Metals Exchange (US); The Royal Mint, BullionVault, Hatton Garden Metals, Atkinsons Bullion (UK); Heraeus, Umicore, PAMP, Argor-Heraeus (EU refiners). Always compare premium over USD spot before purchasing.

Does the weight on a gold bar include the assay card?

No. The stated weight on a gold bar refers only to the gold content. The assay card, packaging and capsule add negligible weight and have no gold value. Always verify the bar weight matches the certificate and that the bar’s serial number matches the assay card.

Which currency does this calculator use?

USD is the primary display currency — the world’s reserve currency and the standard quote for the global LBMA gold market. GBP, EUR and INR conversions appear alongside every USD figure and update live using ECB reference rates from the Frankfurter API.
