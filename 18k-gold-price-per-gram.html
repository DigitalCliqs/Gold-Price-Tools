What Is 18K Gold?

18 karat gold (also written 18ct, 18kt, or 750 gold) contains 75% pure gold — 18 parts gold out of 24 total parts. The remaining 25% is made up of silver, copper, palladium, or zinc alloys. The hallmark "750" found on European 18K pieces denotes 750 parts per thousand (75%) pure gold.

18K is the most popular gold purity for fine jewellery in Europe and is widely used in high-end jewellery worldwide. It is preferred by luxury brands such as Cartier, Bulgari, and Tiffany for its rich colour, superior lustre, and excellent wearability.

Why Choose 18K Gold?

  • Rich colour: The higher gold content (75%) gives 18K a deeper, warmer yellow colour than 14K or 10K gold.
  • Hypoallergenic: 18K white gold and 18K rose gold are among the most skin-friendly jewellery options, with lower alloy content meaning fewer potential allergens.
  • Prestige and value: 18K is the minimum purity used by most luxury jewellery houses. It commands a premium over 14K and 10K due to its higher gold content.
  • Versatility: Available in yellow, white, and rose gold alloys — all at the same 75% gold purity.

18K Gold Hallmarks Explained

  • 18K or 18kt — US and international standard
  • 750 — European standard (Italy, Germany, France, UK)
  • 18ct — British standard ("ct" = carat, same as karat)
  • 0.750 — decimal purity mark on some certified pieces

How to Calculate 18K Gold Value Per Gram

Formula: (Gold spot price per troy oz ÷ 31.1035) × 0.75

Example at $3,300/oz: $3,300 ÷ 31.1035 = $106.10/g × 0.75 = $79.58 per gram of 18K gold
