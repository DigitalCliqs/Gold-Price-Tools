What is 22K gold?

22K gold is an alloy containing 22 parts pure gold out of 24, giving it a purity of 91.67%. It is the dominant gold alloy for jewellery across India, Pakistan, Bangladesh and the Gulf states. The remaining 8.33% is typically copper (giving the warm yellow tone) plus traces of silver or zinc for workability. Items carry the 916 hallmark in the UK and BIS-mark India, or simply 22K / 22KT in North America.

What does the 916 hallmark mean?

The 916 hallmark indicates an item is made of 22 karat gold. The number represents parts pure gold per thousand — 916 parts of every 1,000 are pure gold, or 91.67%. In the UK this stamp is enforced by the four Assay Offices under the Hallmarking Act 1973; in India the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) operates the equivalent hallmarking scheme.

The 916 hallmark explained

In the UK, all gold items above a certain weight must be independently tested and stamped by one of the four UK Assay Offices under the Hallmarking Act 1973. The 916 stamp guarantees the item contains at least 91.67% pure gold. It is illegal under UK law to describe or sell an item as gold if it does not carry an approved hallmark. In India, BIS hallmarking has been mandatory on retail gold jewellery since June 2021.

22K gold colours: yellow, rose & coin alloy

The alloy composition of 22K gold varies depending on the colour. All three carry the same 916 hallmark and contain 91.67% pure gold — the difference is in the other 8.33%.

Why 22K dominates South Asian bridal traditions

In India, Pakistan, Bangladesh and Sri Lanka, gold jewellery is far more than decoration — it is a financial asset, cultural symbol, and integral part of marriage. South Asian families have traditionally stored wealth in 22K gold jewellery for generations. There are specific cultural reasons 22K dominates this market:

  • Streedhan (women's wealth) — In Hindu tradition, gold gifted to a bride is her legal property (streedhan), independent of her husband's assets. This is codified in the Hindu Succession Act. A typical middle-class Indian wedding involves 30–100g of 22K gold jewellery; high-net-worth weddings can involve several hundred grams.
  • Colour and prestige — 22K gold has a deep, rich yellow colour culturally prized across South Asia. Lower karats (14K, 10K) are considered too pale and are associated with Western markets — they sell at a steep discount to face value in India.
  • Near-pure investment — At 91.67% purity, 22K jewellery retains excellent melt value. Families view bridal gold as a portable savings account that can be liquidated in emergencies, often within hours at any souk or bullion dealer.
  • Making charges — Indian and Gulf jewellers charge a "making charge" (fabrication fee) of 8–20% above the gold weight price. When buying 22K jewellery, always negotiate the making charge separately from the gold rate. Get a receipt that itemises gold weight, gold rate, and making charge.
Hardness comparison
22K gold: ~50–80 HV (Vickers) · 18K gold: ~120–150 HV · 14K gold: ~150–180 HV · Pure 24K: ~25 HV. 22K is markedly softer than 18K. For daily-wear rings exposed to abrasion, an 18K alternative may last longer; for necklaces, earrings and ceremonial sets, 22K is excellent.

Seasonal demand: Diwali, Akshaya Tritiya & wedding season

India is the world's second-largest gold consumer (after China), and 22K demand follows predictable seasonal patterns that move global gold prices. The chart above shows the typical 12-month rhythm of Indian gold buying:

  • Diwali (October–November) — The festival of lights is the most auspicious time to buy gold. Indian gold imports typically spike 30–50% in the weeks before Diwali. Dhanteras, the day two days before Diwali, sees an estimated 30–40 tonnes of gold purchased in 24 hours.
  • Akshaya Tritiya (April–May) — A Hindu festival believed to bring perpetual prosperity. Gold purchases on this single day frequently exceed 20 tonnes nationally — another seasonal demand peak that ripples into global spot prices.
  • Wedding season (November–February) — India hosts approximately 10 million weddings per year, with peak season running from late November through February. Each wedding typically involves significant 22K gold purchases.
  • Monsoon dip (June–July) — Demand falls sharply during the monsoon as rural farmers wait for the harvest cash cycle. This is historically the cheapest time of year to buy 22K in India.

For UK and US-based buyers and sellers, understanding these seasonal patterns helps time purchases and sales. Global gold prices often rise slightly in September–October as Indian demand builds ahead of Diwali — sellers can capture better USD pricing into that window.

22K Gold Sovereign & Britannia coins

The UK's Royal Mint Gold Sovereign is one of the world's most recognised 22K gold coins. First minted in 1489, the modern Sovereign contains 7.322g of pure gold (7.988g total weight at 22K purity). Key facts for buyers and sellers:

  • CGT exemption — Gold Sovereigns are UK legal tender and are therefore exempt from Capital Gains Tax (CGT). This makes them one of the most tax-efficient ways to hold physical gold in the UK — particularly powerful for stacking under the £3,000 annual allowance limits that bullion coins bypass entirely.
  • VAT exemption — As investment-grade gold coins, Sovereigns are also exempt from VAT under EU/UK gold investment regulations.
  • Premium over spot — Sovereigns typically sell for 5–15% above their gold melt value, depending on year, condition, and rarity. Proof and limited-edition Sovereigns can command 50–200%+ premiums.
  • Liquidity — Sovereigns are among the most liquid gold coins in the world. Any reputable dealer in the UK, Europe, or Middle East will buy them at competitive rates near melt.

For those considering 22K gold as an investment, Sovereigns offer a superior option compared to jewellery — lower premiums, tax advantages, and universal recognition. Compare with bullion options via our gold bar value calculator.

Selling 22K gold: what to expect

22K gold occupies a unique position in the global secondhand market. Unlike 9K or 10K — which most buyers treat as scrap only — 22K pieces frequently retain value beyond melt thanks to design and cultural demand:

  • Indian and Gulf jewellers — Will typically buy back 22K at 92–98% of melt value, the highest payout percentage of any common karat. Refining costs are minimal because the alloy is already near-pure.
  • UK / US scrap dealers — Generally offer 85–92% of melt for plain 22K scrap. Western refiners price more conservatively than South Asian buyers because their downstream demand is industrial rather than re-fabrication.
  • Branded designer 22K — Pieces from Tanishq, Joyalukkas, Malabar Gold or Damas in the Gulf can resell at small premiums to melt at the same brand's stores.
  • Sovereign and Britannia coins — Sell for 95–100% of spot at most UK bullion dealers; proof or low-mintage years can command 50%+ premiums.

Before selling any 22K item, weigh it on a calibrated 0.01g scale, check the calculator above for the live USD melt baseline, then get at least three quotes. See our guide on where to sell gold safely for vetted dealer recommendations.

How to calculate the 22K gold price per gram

The formula for the 22K gold price per gram is straightforward:

22K price per gram = (Spot price per troy oz ÷ 31.1035) × 0.9167

For example, if the gold spot price is $3,300 per troy ounce:

  • Price per gram of pure gold = $3,300 ÷ 31.1035 = $106.10
  • 22K price per gram = $106.10 × 0.9167 = $97.26

This is the melt value — the intrinsic gold content value. Jewellers, dealers, and pawnbrokers typically pay between 85–95% of melt value when buying 22K (higher than for lower karats because refiners prefer high-purity scrap). When buying at retail, jewellers add a making charge of 8–20% above the gold weight price for fabrication, plus shop margin.

Frequently asked questions

How do I convert the 22K gold price from troy ounces to grams?
There are 31.1035 grams in one troy ounce. Divide the spot price (in any currency) by 31.1035 to get the price per gram of pure gold, then multiply by 0.9167 to get the 22K price per gram. Our live calculator above does this automatically using real-time LBMA spot data, with USD as the primary currency.
What's the difference between 22K solid, gold-filled and gold-plated?
Solid 22K gold contains 91.67% pure gold throughout the item. Gold-filled has a layer of 22K gold mechanically bonded to a base metal core — it contains far less gold by weight (typically 5%). Gold-plated items have only a thin electroplated layer of gold, usually less than 0.05% of the item's total weight. Only solid 22K gold carries the 916 hallmark and has significant melt value.
Why does the 22K gold price vary between different websites?
Small differences between sites reflect the timing of their data feed, the spot price source used (LBMA, COMEX, or OTC), and whether they include FX conversion at slightly different timestamps. GoldPriceTools fetches live USD spot prices from gold-api.com (LBMA-sourced) and currency rates from the Frankfurter API (ECB reference rates), both updated every 60 seconds — applied uniformly across every page on the site for consistency.
Why is 22K gold preferred in India and the Middle East?
22K is preferred for its deep, rich yellow colour, its high melt value (91.67% pure), and its cultural significance. In India gold is gifted at weddings as streedhan — the bride's personal wealth — and in Gulf states 22K gold souks are central to celebrations and household savings. Lower karats like 14K are considered too pale in these markets and are difficult to resell.
Is 22K gold too soft for everyday jewellery?
22K is softer than 14K or 18K (50–80 HV vs 120–180 HV) because of its higher gold content, but it is still worn daily across South Asia and the Middle East. For rings or bracelets subject to heavy abrasion, some jewellers recommend 18K. For necklaces, earrings, mangalsutras and ceremonial pieces, 22K is excellent and the cultural norm.
What is the making charge on 22K gold jewellery?
Making charges (fabrication fees) on 22K gold jewellery typically range from 8–20% above the gold weight price. Machine-made pieces have lower making charges (8–12%); intricate handcrafted designs can reach 15–25%. Always negotiate the making charge separately from the gold rate, and ask for an itemised receipt showing gold weight, gold rate per gram, making charge, and GST/VAT separately.
Is 22K gold real gold?
Yes. 22K gold is real solid gold — it contains 91.67% pure gold (22 parts gold out of 24), alloyed with copper and a small amount of silver. It is hallmarked 916 in Europe/UK and BIS-India, or stamped 22K/22KT in North America. This is the same purity used by the British Royal Mint for the Sovereign coin.
