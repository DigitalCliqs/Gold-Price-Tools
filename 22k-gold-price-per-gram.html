What does 916 mean on gold?

How to Calculate the 22K Gold Price Per Gram

The formula for the 22K gold price per gram is straightforward:

22K price per gram = (Spot price per troy oz ÷ 31.1035) × 0.9167

For example, if the gold spot price is $3,300 per troy ounce:

  • Price per gram of pure gold = $3,300 ÷ 31.1035 = $106.10
  • 22K price per gram = $106.10 × 0.9167 = $97.26

This is the melt value — the intrinsic gold content value. Jewellers, dealers, and pawnbrokers typically pay between 70%–95% of melt value when buying, depending on condition and local market demand. When selling, retailers add a premium of 10%–30% above melt value.

The 916 Hallmark Explained

In the UK, all gold items above a certain weight must be independently tested and stamped by one of the four UK Assay Offices (London, Birmingham, Sheffield, or Edinburgh) under the Hallmarking Act 1973. The 916 hallmark guarantees that the item contains at least 91.67% pure gold. It is illegal under UK law to describe or sell an item as gold if it does not carry an approved hallmark.

On older or imported items, you may also see the hallmark written as .916 or stamped alongside a date letter and assay office mark. When appraising estate jewellery, always check for the 916 stamp before calculating value.

Factors That Affect the 22K Gold Price

The live 22K gold price per gram changes continuously based on the underlying gold spot price, which is driven by several macroeconomic factors:

  • US Dollar strength — Gold is priced globally in USD; a stronger dollar typically pushes spot prices lower, and vice versa.
  • Central bank buying — Central banks (particularly in China, India, and Turkey) have been significant net buyers of gold since 2022, supporting prices.
  • Inflation and interest rates — Gold is widely held as a hedge against inflation. When real interest rates are low or negative, gold becomes relatively more attractive.
  • Geopolitical risk — Gold is a traditional safe-haven asset. Periods of geopolitical tension typically see increased demand and higher prices.
  • Jewellery demand — India and China account for over 50% of global gold jewellery demand. Seasonal festivals such as Diwali and Lunar New Year create demand spikes.
  • GBP/USD exchange rate — For UK buyers and sellers, the pound-dollar rate directly affects the GBP price of gold regardless of the USD spot price.

Selling 22K Gold in the UK

When selling 22K gold jewellery or scrap in the UK, the amount you receive depends on the dealer's buy rate, which is expressed as a percentage of spot melt value. Reputable UK gold buyers typically offer:

  • Online gold buyers (e.g. Hatton Garden Metals, Gold Buyers UK): 85%–95% of melt value
  • High-street jewellers: 70%–85% of melt value
  • Pawnbrokers: 50%–70% of melt value
  • Cash4Gold-style postal services: 40%–65% of melt value

Is 22K Gold a Good Investment?

Investment-grade gold is typically 22K or 24K bullion, where the premium over spot is lowest. 22K gold jewellery carries a fabrication premium that you will not recover on resale — you will generally receive melt value only. However, 22K gold can still serve as a store of value over long time horizons, since the underlying gold content tracks the gold price.

For pure investment purposes, gold ETFs, LBMA-accredited bullion bars, or Royal Mint gold coins (which are VAT-exempt in the UK) typically offer better value than jewellery. For jewellery that you intend to wear and potentially resell, 22K gold at 91.67% purity represents a reasonable balance of value and durability.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I convert the 22K gold price from troy ounces to grams?

There are 31.1035 grams in one troy ounce. Divide the spot price (in any currency) by 31.1035 to get the price per gram of pure gold, then multiply by 0.9167 to get the 22K price per gram. Our live calculator above does this automatically.

What is the difference between 22K gold and gold-filled or gold-plated?

Solid 22K gold contains 91.67% pure gold throughout the item. Gold-filled has a layer of 22K gold mechanically bonded to a base metal core — it contains far less gold by weight. Gold-plated items have only a thin electroplated layer of gold, typically less than 0.05% of the item's total weight. Only solid 22K gold carries the 916 hallmark and has significant melt value.

Why does the 22K gold price per gram vary between different websites?

Small differences between sites reflect the timing of their data feed, the spot price source used (LBMA, COMEX, or OTC), and whether they include the GBP/USD exchange rate at slightly different timestamps.
