GoldPriceTools Editorial Team

The GoldPriceTools Editorial Team is the in-house group responsible for every page on this site. We publish under an organisational byline rather than personal names so that responsibility for accuracy sits clearly with GoldPriceTools as a publisher, and so that individual contributors are not personally exposed online — a deliberate choice in line with UK GDPR and our privacy policy.

What we cover

Our remit is the live UK precious-metals market: gold and silver spot prices, karat purity (9K, 10K, 14K, 18K, 22K, 24K), British silver standards (.800, .900, .925), hallmarking, scrap gold valuation, Royal Mint coinage, and the UK tax treatment of investment-grade bullion under HMRC rules. We focus on practical calculator tools and explanatory content for buyers, sellers and small investors based in the UK.

Where our data comes from

  • Spot prices — sourced live from gold-api.com, which provides LBMA-referenced bid/ask quotes. Prices on tool pages auto-refresh; the dateModified on each page reflects the date of its most recent human editorial review, not the price tick.
  • Bullion market context — the London Bullion Market Association for fix prices and market structure, and the World Gold Council for demand and supply data.
  • UK tax guidance — HMRC for capital gains, VAT exemption rules on investment gold, and the legal-tender CGT exemption applicable to UK Royal Mint coins.
  • Coin & bar specifications — The Royal Mint for British Sovereigns, Britannias and bullion bars; manufacturer datasheets for non-UK products.

Our review process

Every published page goes through a four-step review before it is permitted to go live: (1) outline against a primary source, (2) draft with AI assistance under editorial direction, (3) human fact-check against the cited primary source, and (4) on-page schema and link audit. Tool pages additionally have their calculator output spot-checked against an independent calculation. Pages are re-reviewed on at least an annual cadence, or sooner if a primary source publishes a material update.

Use of AI assistance

Drafts are produced with the help of large language models (currently Claude and Perplexity). No page is published without human editorial review and fact-checking against the primary sources listed above. We disclose this openly because we think it is the honest position; AI-assisted drafting under human editorial control is, in our view, fully consistent with Google’s helpful-content guidance.

Corrections, contact & feedback

If you spot a factual error, a stale figure, or a calculator that disagrees with your independent maths, please tell us via our contact form. We log every reported correction and update the affected page with a revised dateModified.
