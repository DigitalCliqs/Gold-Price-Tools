Where to Sell Gold & Silver in 2026 — Best Options Compared

By GoldPriceTools Editorial Team Updated 17 May 2026 18 min read

With gold at historic highs and silver tracking close behind, 2026 is one of the most favourable selling windows in a generation. But how much ends up in your pocket depends almost entirely on where you sell. The same 14K chain can net $940 at a pawn shop — or $1,600+ at a specialist dealer.

The Quick Answer

For coins & bars, sell to online bullion dealers (APMEX, JM Bullion, BullionByPost) — expect 95–99% of spot. For jewellery & scrap, use specialist precious metal dealers (70–90% of melt). For antiques and designer pieces, auction houses or specialist resellers beat melt by multiples. Avoid pawn shops and mall kiosks — they typically pay 25–60% of melt value.

Is It a Good Time to Sell Gold and Silver in 2026?

Short answer: yes, by almost any metric. Gold hit its highest-ever recorded price in March 2026 — and while prices have softened slightly from that peak as markets consolidate, the underlying demand picture remains strongly supportive. The World Gold Council noted that gold had achieved over 50 all-time highs in 2025 alone before carrying that momentum into 2026. Silver has tracked gold with even sharper moves in percentage terms.

The structural case for high precious metal prices — central bank accumulation, de-dollarisation, persistent geopolitical uncertainty, and elevated inflation expectations — has not materially changed. Even if prices dip modestly from current levels, anyone who acquired gold or silver before 2023 is sitting on significant gains.

A piece of 18K jewellery worth $90/g in melt value two years ago might now be worth $130–$140/g — without you doing a thing.

For sellers, the implication is straightforward: the higher the spot price, the more every gram is worth. If you have inherited jewellery you will never wear, duplicate coins, or scrap metal collecting dust, converting it at today's prices is rational financial decision-making.

i

That said, "should I sell?" is personal. If you hold gold as a long-term inflation hedge or portfolio anchor, the case for keeping it remains strong. This guide focuses on getting maximum value from the metal you've already decided to part with.

Know Your Metal's Value Before You Sell

The single most important thing you can do before approaching any buyer is to understand what your metal is actually worth. This removes any information asymmetry between you and the person making an offer — and makes it immediately obvious when you are being lowballed.

STEP 01

Check the live spot price

The gold spot price is the global benchmark — the price at which one troy ounce of pure gold trades right now. Use live tools updated every 60 seconds.

STEP 02

Calculate your melt value

This is the intrinsic gold (or silver) content converted to dollars. Use weight, purity, and the current spot price per gram.

STEP 03

Understand "% of spot"

No buyer pays 100% of melt. The question is how big the margin is — 95–98% for top bullion dealers, 25–40% for pawn shops.

★ Melt Value Formula
Weight (g) × Purity × Spot Price / g
Example: a 20-gram, 18K gold bracelet at $150/g for pure 24K gold →
20 × 0.75 × $150 = $2,250 melt value

Online calculators on sites like GoldPriceTools' scrap gold calculator allow you to input the karat (9K, 10K, 14K, 18K, 22K, 24K) and weight directly for an instant figure. Knowing your melt value before any conversation gives you the power to evaluate every offer on its merits and walk away when it falls short.

Selling Gold Coins: A Special Consideration

Gold coins have two potential sources of value: bullion value (gold content at spot) and numismatic value (collector premium from rarity, condition, history). Knowing which you hold determines where you should sell.

Bullion

Standard Bullion Coins

American Eagles, Canadian Maples, Krugerrands, Kangaroos, Philharmonics. Best sold to specialist bullion dealers who recognise their global liquidity. Most pay 95–99% of spot in good condition.

Numismatic

Rare & Graded Coins

1933 Gold Eagles, key-date Victorian sovereigns, graded slabs. Sell to numismatic specialists or auction houses. Selling a rare key-date to a scrap buyer could cost you hundreds or thousands in unrealised value.

✓

Tip for selling gold coins near you: Local coin dealers (LCS) offer same-day assessment and payment. Offers vary considerably — compare two or three quotes before committing.

Selling Silver: Coins, Bullion, and Silverware

The silver-selling process mirrors gold, with a few important distinctions. Spot value per gram is lower than gold, so processing costs represent a larger percentage — payouts as a % of melt are typically lower for silver scrap than gold scrap.

Bullion

Silver Bullion Coins

Silver Eagles, Britannias, Maples, Philharmonics — sell to bullion dealers. BullionByPost quotes 100.1% of spot for recognised silver coins; most online buyers pay 95–100%.

Scrap

Sterling Silver (925)

Sterling jewellery and silverware. Specialist scrap buyers and online silver buying services typically pay 70–85% of melt value. Always check hallmarks first.

Antique

Silver Flatware & Hollowware

Sets with good hallmarks, maker's marks, and complete service condition command significant premiums at auction over melt. Check hallmarks before assuming it's plate.

Plated

Silver-Plated (EPNS)

Almost no silver value. Sell as decorative objects, not for metal. Items without "925", "Sterling", or a lion passant hallmark are almost certainly silver-plated.

!

Sterling silver vs silver plate: Sterling silver is always hallmarked with "925," "Sterling," or in the UK, a lion passant and date letter. Items without these marks are almost certainly silver-plated and contain negligible silver value. Do not confuse the two before approaching a buyer.

How to Sell Gold and Silver Online: Step-by-Step

For those choosing an online dealer or mail-in service (the route that typically maximises payout), the process is consistent across reputable providers.

  1. Research and select a dealer Check Trustpilot, Google Reviews, and the BBB. Look for BNTA (British Numismatic Trade Association), PNG (Professional Numismatists Guild), or equivalent national trade association membership as a baseline credential check.
  2. Get an online quote Most reputable dealers have online calculators or quote forms. Enter the karat/purity, weight, and item type for an indicative price.
  3. Lock in your price When you are ready to proceed, call or complete the online checkout to lock a live price. Prices are typically locked for 24–48 hours to account for shipping time. JM Bullion allows live price locks online 24/7.
  4. Package and ship securely Use a strong outer box with bubble wrap or foam padding — items should not rattle. Always use registered, tracked, insured post. Keep your proof of postage until payment clears. Many dealers provide pre-paid labels.
  5. Receive and accept the final offer The dealer tests and verifies your items upon receipt. The confirmed offer is usually within a few percent of the quote for standard items. Reputable dealers always contact you if the final assessment differs significantly before proceeding.
  6. Get paid Payment is typically made by bank transfer within 1–3 business days of item receipt and verification.

Tax Considerations When Selling Gold and Silver

Tax treatment varies by country, and getting this wrong can result in an unexpected tax bill. The core principles below are consistent across most developed markets, but always consult a qualified tax adviser before significant sales.

Capital gains treatment

In most countries, profit from selling gold and silver is subject to capital gains tax (CGT). The gain = selling price − original cost ("cost basis"). Rates and thresholds vary significantly:

  • United States: gold held more than one year is taxed as a collectible at a maximum federal rate of 28%.
  • United Kingdom: CGT annual allowance is £3,000 (2024/25 tax year); gains above this are taxable at 18–24% depending on income level.

Important exemptions

★

UK Royal Mint coins are CGT-exempt. Gold Britannias, Gold Sovereigns (post-1837), Silver Britannias, and other Royal Mint legal tender series are exempt from CGT regardless of the gain realised — one of the most significant tax advantages available to UK precious metal investors.

  • In many jurisdictions, selling within tax-advantaged accounts (Roth IRA, ISA, SIPP) eliminates CGT on gold held within those structures.
  • VAT: Investment-grade gold (99.5%+ purity bars and coins) is exempt from VAT in the EU and UK. Silver typically carries VAT when sold new; second-hand silver under the Margin Scheme can be effectively VAT-free.

How to sell tax-efficiently

  • Prioritise selling CGT-exempt coins (where applicable) before taxable bars or non-legal-tender coins.
  • Where gains exceed your annual allowance, spread sales across two tax years.
  • Offset capital gains with any capital losses from other assets in the same tax year.
  • Keep accurate records of purchase price, date, and associated costs (insurance, storage) — these reduce your taxable gain.

Red Flags and Scams to Avoid

The precious metals buying industry is largely honest and professional at the reputable end of the market — but it attracts bad actors targeting sellers who don't know the value of what they hold. Watch for these red flags.

!

Door-to-door gold buyers

High-pressure sales technique targeting vulnerable sellers. There is no legitimate reason for a gold buyer to operate this way — always decline.

!

No price lock or return guarantee

Mail-in services that encourage you to send items before providing a firm offer can make returns expensive and complicated. Lock in advance.

!

"Cash-for-gold" pop-up events

Hotel and shopping centre events pay 30–50% of melt and create artificial time pressure to prevent comparison shopping. Avoid.

!

Mixing karats before weighing

A dishonest buyer combines your 18K and 9K pieces, weighing together at the lower-karat price. Always insist on separate weighing by purity.

!

Pressure to decide immediately

Any legitimate buyer gives you time to compare offers. Urgency = manipulation. Walk away from anyone who won't let you think.

!

No physical address or unclear credentials

Reputable dealers display business registration, trade association membership, and contactable addresses. Anonymous buyers are a red flag.

Tips to Maximise What You Receive

Eight practical actions that compound to a meaningfully better payout — often the difference between 60% and 90% of melt value.

  1. Know your melt value before any conversation. Use a live price calculator with your weight and karat before approaching any buyer.
  2. Get at least three quotes. Offers for the same piece can vary by 20–30% between buyers. Multiple quotes cost nothing and give you market truth.
  3. Sort by purity. Separate gold by karat before presenting. Mixed lots let unscrupulous buyers average down; sorted items get priced correctly.
  4. Sell bullion to bullion dealers, scrap to scrap dealers. A pawn shop is a poor venue for a PAMP Suisse bar; a scrap dealer is a poor venue for plain broken jewellery. Match channel to asset.
  5. Consider timing. Selling when spot is high — as in 2026 — maximises dollar value per gram. Set price alerts to track spot movements.
  6. Check for numismatic value before scrapping coins. Any coin dated before 1933 or with unusual design should be checked with a specialist first. The difference can be enormous.
  7. Use insured, tracked shipping. For any mail-in sale, declared-value insurance protects against loss in transit. Keep proof of postage until payment clears.
  8. Read the fine print. Before sending anything, read the dealer's terms on price locks, return policies, and payment timelines. Reputable dealers are transparent upfront.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is the best place to sell gold?

For investment-grade bullion coins and bars, online bullion dealers (APMEX, JM Bullion, BullionByPost, SD Bullion) consistently offer the best rates — 95–99% of spot. For jewellery and scrap, specialist precious metal dealers offer the best combination of fair pricing, speed, and professional testing.

How do I find gold buyers near me?

Search for "precious metal dealers" or "coin dealers" with strong Trustpilot and Google reviews in your area, or ask for recommendations from local collector communities. Avoid anonymous cash-for-gold kiosks and pop-up buyers. National trade association directories (BNTA in the UK, ICTA in the US) list vetted, reputable dealers by location.

What is scrap gold and how do I sell it?

Scrap gold includes any gold jewellery, broken or unwearable pieces, dental gold, or gold items being sold for metal content only — not design or collector value. Scrap is bought at a percentage of its melt value by dealers and refiners. The higher the purity and gold content, the more you receive per gram. Always run the numbers with a scrap gold calculator first.

How do I sell gold coins near me?

Local coin dealers (LCS) offer same-day assessment and payment for most bullion and numismatic coins. For bullion, online dealers typically offer better rates than local shops for common coins; for rare or collectible coins, a local specialist who knows the numismatic market may provide a better result. Always get 2–3 quotes.

Can I sell silver for cash?

Yes — specialist dealers, bullion buyback services, and local coin dealers all buy silver coins, bars, and jewellery. For sterling silver (925) jewellery and silverware, specialist online buyers often offer better rates than local dealers. Silver-plated items (EPNS, silver-plated cutlery) have almost no silver value and cannot be sold as silver.

How do I sell gold and silver tax-free?

Tax rules vary by jurisdiction. In the UK, selling CGT-exempt coins (Gold and Silver Britannias, Gold Sovereigns from the Royal Mint) generates no CGT regardless of profit. In the US, using a Roth IRA or similar tax-advantaged account structure can eliminate capital gains tax on qualifying disposals. Staying within your annual CGT allowance is another route. Always consult a tax professional for advice specific to your situation.

How do I sell gold online safely?

Use only established, reviewed dealers with transparent pricing and clear return policies. Check Trustpilot and Google reviews. Always use tracked, insured shipping with declared value. Keep proof of postage. Lock your price before shipping and confirm the dealer's process for returns if you decline the final offer.

Where can I sell gold bars?

Gold bars — particularly LBMA-accredited bars from refiners like PAMP, Valcambi, and Perth Mint — are best sold to specialist bullion dealers who recognise their provenance and can offer rates close to spot. Banks rarely buy gold bars directly from private sellers; specialist dealers and online bullion buyback services are the primary route for most private individuals.

Do pawn shops pay good money for gold?

Pawn shops typically pay only 25–60% of melt value for gold jewellery — among the lowest rates in the market. The same 14K ring weighing 5 grams could net $118 at a pawn shop versus $400+ at a specialist dealer. Pawn shops offer speed and immediate cash, but at a steep cost. Only use them when you need cash in your hands within the hour and have no alternative.
