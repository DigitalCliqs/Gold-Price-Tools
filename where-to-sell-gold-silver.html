Where to Sell Gold and Silver in 2026 — Best Options Compared

By GoldPriceTools Editorial Team · Updated April 30, 2026

You can sell gold and silver through online bullion dealers, local coin shops, pawn shops, jewellers, or refineries. Online dealers typically offer the best prices (closest to spot), while pawn shops offer convenience but pay 20–40% below spot. The best option depends on the form of your metal (jewellery, coins, bars, or scrap).

Selling Online

Selling your precious metals online is often the best way to secure a payout closest to the spot price. Major online bullion dealers such as JM Bullion, APMEX, GoldSilver.com, and Kitco have dedicated buyback programmes. These companies handle high volumes, allowing them to operate on narrower margins and offer better prices than local shops.

The process typically involves locking in a price over the phone or online, shipping your metals via insured mail, and receiving payment via bank transfer or cheque within 3 to 7 days.

Selling Locally

If you need cash immediately or prefer face-to-face transactions, selling locally is your best option. Local coin shops are generally the most reputable and offer competitive prices for bullion coins and small bars (typically 80–92% of spot).

Jewellers and goldsmiths are better suited for selling jewellery, offering 60–80% of the melt value. Pawn shops should be considered a last resort; while they offer ultimate convenience, they usually pay the lowest rates (50–70% of spot) due to their high overheads and need for higher profit margins.

Selling Scrap Gold

When selling broken jewellery, dental gold, or unhallmarked items, you are selling "scrap." The value is based entirely on the metal content, not aesthetics or craftsmanship. To ensure you don't get ripped off, it is critical to get at least 3 quotes from different buyers (such as local jewellers and online refineries).

Before soliciting quotes, always use a scrap gold calculator to determine the true melt value of your items. If you are selling items of varying purities, you should also check the current 14K gold price per gram and other specific karat values.

Selling Silver Coins

Silver coins can be sold to online dealers or local coin shops. The payout depends heavily on whether the coins are sovereign bullion (like American Silver Eagles), which command a premium, or generic silver rounds. Numismatic (collectible) coins should be appraised by a specialist, as their value can far exceed their silver content. You can calculate the silver melt value of coins to have a baseline understanding.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where is the best place to sell gold?

Online bullion dealers generally offer the best payouts for coins and bars (90-97% of spot), while refineries pay the most for scrap gold (90-95%). For small amounts of jewellery, a local coin shop or independent jeweller will typically pay 80-92%.

How much should I expect to get when selling silver?

Expect to receive around 90-95% of the spot price for sovereign silver coins (like Silver Eagles or Maples) from online dealers. Generic silver rounds or bars usually fetch 85-90%, while scrap silver or flatware often only yields 70-80% of melt value.

Do pawn shops pay good money for gold?

Pawn shops should be a last resort. They typically offer the lowest payouts, ranging from 50% to 70% of the actual gold spot value, because they have high overhead costs and act as intermediaries.

How do I know I'm getting a fair price for scrap gold?

Always calculate your gold's melt value first using a scrap gold calculator. Get quotes from at least three different buyers, and expect a fair offer to be between 80% and 92% of the total melt value.
