Gold has powered to multi-month highs, yet gold-mining shares have lagged once
again — a familiar frustration for investors who expected leverage to the metal.

## What's holding miners back

Two things. **Cost inflation** — labour, energy and equipment — has eaten into
margins even as the gold price climbed. And after years of value destruction,
management teams are prioritising **capital discipline and dividends** over
aggressive expansion, which caps the operating leverage investors once enjoyed.

## Could that change?

If gold holds these levels, margins should expand and free cash flow could surprise
to the upside — a setup that has historically preceded periods of miner
outperformance. The catch is that investors need to see it in the numbers first.
