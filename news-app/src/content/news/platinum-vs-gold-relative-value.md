Platinum trades at a steep discount to gold — near the widest in the metal's
history. For contrarian investors, that gap is starting to look interesting.

## Why the discount exists

Platinum is heavily tied to industrial and autocatalyst demand, which has been
pressured by the shift to electric vehicles. Gold, by contrast, has benefited from
investment and central-bank flows. The result is a historic spread.

## The contrarian case

Supply is tightening as mine output falls, and hydrogen-economy demand could
provide a new structural source of buying. If the discount mean-reverts even
partway, platinum's upside relative to gold is meaningful.

## The risk

Relative-value trades can stay cheap for years. This is a patient position, not a
momentum play.
