Central banks added roughly **290 tonnes of gold** to their reserves in the second
quarter, keeping official-sector demand near record levels. The buying has been a
structural pillar of the gold market for three years running.

## Who's buying

Emerging-market central banks led the way, continuing a multi-year push to
diversify reserves away from the US dollar. Gold's appeal as a neutral reserve
asset — with no counterparty and no issuer — has only grown amid geopolitical
uncertainty.

## Why it matters for prices

Official-sector buying is **price-insensitive and persistent**: banks accumulate
on a schedule rather than chasing momentum. That steady bid puts a floor under the
market and helps explain gold's resilience even when real yields tick higher.
