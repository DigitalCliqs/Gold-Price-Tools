A quick run-through of the week in precious metals — what moved, why, and what to
keep an eye on.

## The week in 60 seconds

- **Gold** held above $4,500, easing slightly as real yields ticked up but
  finding steady support from central-bank buying.
- **Silver** stole the show, jumping ~4% on industrial and solar demand.
- **The gold-to-silver ratio** narrowed toward 62, a sign of broadening strength.

## Levels to watch next week

Gold support sits at $4,460–$4,480 with resistance near $4,620. For silver, the
key is whether it can hold its breakout. The next inflation print is the macro
event most likely to set the tone.

## Bottom line

A constructive week: gold consolidating, silver leading, and the ratio confirming
the move. Nothing here changes the bigger picture of a resilient precious-metals
bid.
