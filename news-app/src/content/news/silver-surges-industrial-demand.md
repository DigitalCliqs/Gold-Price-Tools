Silver outpaced gold this week, jumping roughly **4%** as a tightening physical
market collided with record demand from solar manufacturers. The move narrowed
the gold-to-silver ratio and reminded investors why the "poor man's gold" can
move twice as fast as bullion in both directions.

## Why silver moved

Unlike gold, silver is **half industrial metal**. Photovoltaic demand has set
fresh records as solar installations accelerate worldwide, and that structural
draw is meeting a market already in a multi-year supply deficit. When investment
demand returns on top of that, prices can gap higher quickly.

## The ratio signal

The gold-to-silver ratio slipped back toward the low 60s. Historically, a falling
ratio signals broadening strength across precious metals and often precedes
silver's strongest runs — though it cuts both ways when sentiment turns.

## What to watch

Watch the next round of solar and industrial data, plus exchange inventories. A
continued draw on above-ground stocks would keep the squeeze narrative alive.
