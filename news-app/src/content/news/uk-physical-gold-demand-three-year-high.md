UK retail demand for physical gold reached a **three-year high**, with the Royal
Mint reporting brisk sales of Sovereigns and Britannias as investors sought a
hedge against sticky inflation.

## What's driving it

Two factors stand out. CGT-exempt British coins remain a tax-efficient way for UK
savers to hold gold, and rising prices have drawn momentum buyers off the
sidelines. Fractional coins and digital gold products have also lowered the entry
point for first-time buyers.

## The takeaway

Strong domestic demand reinforces the broader picture: gold is behaving like a
core portfolio holding for UK investors, not a speculative punt.
